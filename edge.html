02. THE FIVE DISCIPLINES
Discipline 01

Leadership & Followship

Sandhurst Royal Military Academy

"Plans fail on contact with reality. What survives is intent."
  • Mission Command: Give intent, not instructions. The prompts give you what to look for, not what to say.
  • Uncertainty: Every prompt includes "EXPERIENCE SEES" because reality will break the AI's plan.
  • Governance: R/A/G security classifications provide the structure to move fast safely.
RESULT: RAPID DEAL EXECUTION WITH STRICT GOVERNANCE
Discipline 02

Behavioural Science

NLP Practitioner · Clinical Hypnotherapy

"People decide based on belief systems, then seek evidence to rationalise."
  • Epistemology: Decoding how each stakeholder validates truth (data, relationships, or intuition).
  • 6 Drivers: Safety, Order, Power, Achievement, Affiliation, Sustainability map to limbic responses.
  • Reframing: Objection handling doesn't counter arguments — it changes their meaning.
RESULT: REFRAMES UNSPOKEN OBJECTIONS EARLY
Discipline 03

Strategic Transformation

Harvard Business School — Exec Ed

"Stop thinking about what you're selling. Look from the inside out."
  • The Buyer's Lens: GAIN starts from their boardroom priorities, not your solution.
  • Shareholder Value: The only language that survives is EBITDA, margin, and velocity.
  • Narrative: Transformation is a story told to the market, not a project plan.
RESULT: ELEVATES CONVERSATIONS TO THE C-SUITE
Discipline 04

Intelligence Mapping

HUMINT Doctrine → Commercial Practice

"The political map is invisible — until you learn to look for it systematically."
  • Informal Power: The MAP phase exists because the real power structure is rarely in the chart.
  • Sphere of Influence: Distinguishing what you can control from what you can only shape.
  • Sequencing: Gather → Assess → Influence → Nurture. Intelligence before action.
RESULT: EXPOSES THE SHADOW ORG CHART
Discipline 05

25 Years of Live Deals

£790M Career Portfolio · Enterprise IT Services

"The AI finds the 80%. The last 20% comes from having been in the room when things went wrong."

  • Field Failures: Failure modes on this site were earned through real lost revenue.
  • Execution: A 20-minute executed plan beats a 4-hour perfect plan.
  • Scalability: Preparation is the only competitive edge you can systematise.
