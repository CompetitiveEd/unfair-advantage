Five Disciplines

Mission Command

ADAPTIVE EXECUTION

The common failure: Sellers freeze when the procurement plan breaks. They wait for manager permission to pivot.

The discipline: Commander's intent embedded. The team knows the strategic outcome. Autonomy to adapt dynamically when the friction changes. No waiting for permission. No freezing when the plan breaks.

Applied Behavioural Science

BELIEF ALIGNMENT

The common failure: They pitch logic (ROI, features) to a buyer who is quietly terrified of operational failure or losing their position.

The discipline: Map the behavioural drivers — Safety, Power, Order, Achievement. Don't change their mind. Align the solution to their existing decision heuristics. The buyer feels understood, not sold to.

Structural Negotiation

HARVARD & LBS FRAMEWORKS

The common failure: They give away margin at the end of the quarter because they confuse haggling with negotiating.

The discipline: Engineer the deal architecture early. Trade items of low cost for items of high perceived value. Lock multi-year terms before procurement engages. Margin protected, not surrendered.

Champion Development

INFLUENCE ARCHITECTURE

The common failure: They map standard org charts and assume the CIO makes the decision. They get blindsided by a stakeholder they never identified.

The discipline: Identify internal champions. Arm them with the exact narrative to defend the solution when you're not in the room. The deal advances because someone inside is making the case.

Enterprise Execution Layer

THE HUMAN LAYER

The common failure: They mistake AI-generated data for a closing strategy. They have the intelligence but fail the execution.

The discipline: Data is not a differentiator. Translating structured intelligence into a political, multi-threaded boardroom strategy is the only skill that commands a premium. This cannot be bought or faked — it must be built through repetition across hundreds of live deals.

The Final Constraint: Tools ≠ Talent

You can give a bad golfer an expensive set of clubs — they'll just hit the ball into the woods much further. Giving an average sales team an AI prompt engine without teaching them the behavioural science to interpret the output is identical. The edge is not the prompt. The edge is the judgment of what to do with the output.
