The Objective

Stop selling to job titles. A VP of IT might have the mandate to evaluate, but the CFO holds the pen, and the COO holds the veto. The Assess phase identifies the true Economic Buyer and isolates hidden detractors before they kill your deal from the shadows.

The Psychology

People buy to protect themselves. Mapping influence means mapping fear, ambition, and internal rivalries. When you understand what a stakeholder stands to lose if you win, you can pre-empt their objections before they ever voice them to the board.

Governance

Hallucinating relationships destroys deals. AI must only map documented connections, overriding assumptions with explicit evidence. If the prompt engine cannot verify political capital from the provided data, it must return 'UNKNOWN'. No guessing.

Execution Checklist

  • Export historical email/call transcripts for the account.
  • Aggregate LinkedIn/bio data for top 5 stakeholders.
  • Upload qualitative data to secure LLM session.
  • Run prompts A-01 through A-04 sequentially.
  • Cross-reference the shadow map with your internal sponsor.

Human Checkpoint: Context Override

Do not trust the algorithm's empathy. AI maps the data perfectly, but it cannot hear the hesitation in a VP's voice on a zoom call. AI provides the map; human intuition validates the territory.

A-01

The Detractor Search

▼

Variables Needed

[ACCOUNT_CONTEXT] [OUR_SOLUTION]

Architecture / Purpose

Forces you to look for enemies before friends. Identifies the specific executive whose budget, headcount, or legacy technology is threatened by your implementation.

Engine Logic

Adopts the persona of a ruthless political strategist. Strips away 'corporate synergy' to find raw, self-preserving motivations.

Governance Constraint

If no direct threat can be inferred from the provided context, the model must explicitly state "NO OBVIOUS DETRACTOR IDENTIFIED."

Prompt Code

Act as an expert corporate political strategist. Review the provided [ACCOUNT_CONTEXT] and the details of [OUR_SOLUTION]. 

Your objective is to identify the 'Hidden Detractor'. Based purely on the text provided:
1. Which executive role stands to lose budget, headcount, or operational control if our solution is purchased?
2. Which stakeholder previously championed the incumbent system we are trying to replace?
3. Detail the likely internal narrative this detractor will use to kill our deal at the board level.

Constraint: Base this strictly on the provided context. If evidence is lacking, output "NO OBVIOUS DETRACTOR IDENTIFIED." Do not invent scenarios.
A-02

Economic Buyer Validation

▼

Variables Needed

[STAKEHOLDER_DATA]

Architecture / Purpose

Separates budget administrators from true discretionary spenders. Confirms if the person you are talking to can actually sign the contract.

Engine Logic

Scans communication and role history for evidence of unbudgeted capital release. Discards titles in favor of historic purchasing behavior.

Expected Output

A binary classification: "True Economic Buyer" or "Budget Administrator," followed by the supporting evidence.

Prompt Code

Analyze the provided [STAKEHOLDER_DATA] and communication logs. Evaluate the primary contact.

Determine if they are the true Economic Buyer (can release unbudgeted funds) or a Budget Administrator (can only spend pre-approved budget).
Look for:
1. Language indicating need for secondary approval ("I need to take this to the committee").
2. Evidence of past unilateral purchasing decisions.
3. Alignment of their personal KPIs with the financial outcome of our solution.

Classify the contact and provide 3 bullet points of evidence supporting your conclusion.
A-03

The Champion's Test

▼

Variables Needed

[CHAMPION_COMMUNICATIONS]

Override / Human EQ

A prospect agreeing with you does not make them a champion. Evaluate if they have the political capital to actually drive change.

Prompt Code

Review the [CHAMPION_COMMUNICATIONS]. We need to test if this person is a true champion or merely an enthusiastic 'coach'.

Assess based on these three criteria:
1. Access to Power: Do they readily provide access to the Economic Buyer, or do they block it?
2. Inside Information: Have they shared non-public, asymmetric information regarding the evaluation criteria or competitors?
3. Influence: Can they dictate the next step in the sales process, or are they constantly deferring to a generic 'team'?

Provide a brutally honest assessment. Recommend a specific 'test' we can run in the next meeting to prove their influence.
A-04

The Coalition Map

▼

Prompt Code

Synthesize all provided [ALL_STAKEHOLDERS] data into a 'Shadow Org Chart'. Ignore formal titles.

Map the stakeholders into these specific buckets:
- Veto Powers: Who can kill the deal without explanation?
- The Funding Source: Whose exact budget is being drawn down?
- The Implementation Risk: Who is responsible for the actual deployment and risks failure?
- The Indifferents: Who is mandated to evaluate but doesn't actually care about the outcome?

Output a summary of the 'Voting Bloc'—the exact combination of 3-4 people required to guarantee a 'Yes' at the board level.

Where This Breaks (Failure Modes)

The Title Trap

Believing the C-suite title equals decision-making power. Titles signify authority; mapping shadow hierarchies reveals actual political capital.

The Fake Champion

Mistaking an enthusiastic mid-level user for a political heavyweight. If they can't arrange a meeting with the Economic Buyer, they are a coach, not a champion.

The Ghost Detractor

Ignoring the quiet stakeholder who silently vetos the deal via email after you leave the room. Mapping must uncover what happens when you aren't present.

Stale Maps

Assuming political capital is static. Re-orgs happen, priorities pivot, and influence shifts weekly. A map from Q1 is a liability in Q3.
