THE 90-DAY ARC
DAYS 1-30

GATHER + ASSESS

Intelligence, not action.

The first 30 days are diagnostic. Financial baseline — the real P&L, not the reported one. Account tiering — top 10, contenders, whitespace. Account intelligence on every key relationship — customer trajectory, competitive landscape, pipeline gaps.

Team assessment across three dimensions: License (do people have permission to act?), Capability (do they have the skills and tools?), Motivation (what actually drives them?). Identify the unstructured cost — the invisible waste that's been there so long nobody notices it.

In one diagnostic, sellers gave different answers when asked what the company actually sold. The diagnostic doesn't find surprises. It finds what everyone knows but nobody has written down.

DAYS 31-60

INFLUENCE + BUILD

Crystallise the message. Pick the fight.

Value proposition sharpened to one sentence. Nine customer evidence stories that any seller can deploy in conversation. A go-to-market framework simple enough to go viral — the equivalent of one folded piece of paper that became a £55M business.

Seller accountability and behaviours agreed — not mandated, agreed. Reward architecture aligned to the right outcomes. Then: pick one strategic complex deal. Deploy full GAIN methodology. Arm the champion with a story they can sell when you're not in the room.

If a seller can't explain the framework in two minutes, they won't use it. If a customer can't see themselves in it, they won't buy it. The art is making twenty-five years of complexity look like common sense.

DAYS 61-90

WIN + MEASURE

Evidence, not celebration.

The strategic deal closes — or is measurably advanced. Every seller can pitch the value proposition and deploy the evidence. Pipeline coverage is measurably improved. Win rate on competitive bids is tracked.

The team sees it work on a real deal with real money. Then: plan the next 90 days. Because the first 90 isn't a programme. It's the first cycle of a continuous operating rhythm.

A senior seller used the intelligence mapping and discovered his 'sure-thing' champion had been reorganised out of the decision. Changed strategy. Won. Became the loudest advocate for the methodology. That's how adoption works — not by mandate, by evidence.

WHY THIS WORKS

The Consultant Trap

Mandate a new methodology from the top down. Sales teams ignore it. Adoption fails. CRM fills with fake compliance data. The methodology dies when the consultant leaves.

Why it fails: sellers see it as management theatre. It takes months to roll out. Results take longer. The only people who comply are the ones who didn't need help.

Adoption by Results

Use intelligence to win a deal the team didn't expect to win. The person next to the winner asks how. Adoption is pulled, not pushed.

Why it works: success creates demand. It works on the live deal you have today. Real data from real wins. The methodology sustains because the results sustain.

Stop debating. Start winning.

Take a live deal. Apply the intelligence layer. Win it. Measure the delta. Then decide.
