The 4 Foundational Rules

Best Product Rarely Wins

The market doesn't reward the best feature set. It rewards the vendor who best aligns their solution with the buyer's internal risk and political friction.

Discovery is Dead

Asking executives to educate you on their business is a fireable offense. You must arrive with a hypothesis derived from their public filings, not a questionnaire.

Ego Doesn't Scale

Relying on a few 'hero' reps to close the quarter creates a fragile business. The methodology must be systematised so average performers can execute at a premium level.

AI is a Synthesizer

AI cannot close a deal. It lacks EQ and accountability. It is a research compression engine—freeing up human capital to navigate other humans.

The 3 Eras of Evolution
ERA 01

The Analogue Era

Whiteboards, post-it notes, and endless account planning sessions. The behavioural methodology was pure, but execution was painfully slow. Deep account research took weeks, stalling momentum.

ERA 02

The Portfolio Engine

Pipeline scaled until it broke delivery. That was the point. The problem was no longer trivial — every function had to be rebuilt: vendors, cost models, governance, operations. The deal engine forced the repositioning. £35M to £183M.

ERA 03 (CURRENT)

AI Compression

Injecting LLMs into the intelligence phase. A 3-week research cycle compressed into 20 minutes. The underlying methodology remained identical, but the execution velocity shifted to warp speed.

Where This Breaks (Failure Modes)

The Drift vs IQ+EQ+AI

Letting the AI dictate the strategy. Unchecked, the model drifts into generic platitudes. You must maintain the IQ/EQ human override at all times.

Wrong Context In

Feeding the model marketing collateral instead of raw financial filings. Garbage in equals hallucinated, unusable garbage out.

Over-Steering

Forcing the AI to confirm your bias. If you prompt it to 'prove they need us', it will lie to please you. Ask it to find the friction instead.
