HONEST FAILURES

Where This Breaks

Any methodology that doesn't tell you where it fails is selling you something. AI-assisted intelligence has real limitations. Here are the ones that have cost time, credibility, or both.

Hallucination

AI fabricated a partnership between two companies that never existed. The battle card referenced it as a proof point. If it had reached the client, it would have destroyed credibility in the first meeting.

Caught by: Step 5 capability matching flagged it as "no supporting documentation found." The verification prompt works â€” but only if you run it.

Rule: Never cite an AI-generated fact in a meeting without source verification. If you can't find it in the original document, it doesn't exist.

Over-reliance

Stopped applying judgment. Copied the briefing output directly into an outreach note without editing. The language was technically correct but completely generic â€” it could have been sent to any resilience director at any financial services firm. The recipient ignored it.

What went wrong: The chain generated good intelligence, but the human layer â€” the part that says "this specific sentence will resonate because of what I know about this specific person" â€” was skipped.

Rule: The AI generates 80% of the work. The last 20% â€” the part that makes it personal, specific, and human â€” is yours. That's not a limitation. That's the point.

Transcription Disaster

Voice-to-text during a meeting captured "cost-to-serve" as "cost to surf." The follow-up note referenced the prospect's focus on "cost to surf" three times. They didn't respond.

Rule: Always read AI-generated content out loud before sending. Your ears catch what your eyes skip.

False Confidence

Rule: AI doesn't know what's changed since its sources were published. Always verify leadership, org structure, and recent announcements within 48 hours of your meeting.
