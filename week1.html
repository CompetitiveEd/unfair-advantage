Week 1: The Intelligence Layer

Building the depth that wins trust before you walk in the room.

The Proof: Context Beats Jargon

Tom, a veteran AE, recently lost a deal because the competitor "seemed to understand the business better". They used the exact language the executive team was using internally, while Tom relied on generic vendor value propositions.

The 20-Minute Workflow in Action:

Tom used AI to analyze three years of annual reports and earnings calls. He found "cost-to-serve" mentioned 17 times, while "innovation" appeared only twice.

Outcome: By repositioning his pitch from "digital transformation" to "operational efficiency with managed risk," he won a multi-year contract.

LEVEL 1

Strategic Hierarchy Extraction

Context

Winners talk to Goals, not departments. This prompt maps the connection between board-level targets and the projects you sell into.

Fuel Required CEO Shareholder Letter Annual Report (Front Half)
"Analyze the attached excerpts from [COMPANY].

1. Identify the top 3 board-level GOALS for this fiscal year.
2. Map the STRATEGIES being deployed to reach those goals.
3. Identify the funded INITIATIVES that sit under these strategies.
4. Provide exact page numbers for every finding."

Output Example: RetailCo Ltd

  • GOAL: "Reduce cost-to-serve by 15% over 3 years" (p.4)
  • STRATEGY: "Supply chain optimization through technology modernization"
  • INITIATIVE: "Cloud migration of legacy ERP system" (p.28)
LEVEL 2

The Accusation Audit (Friction Finder)

Context

Based on Chris Voss’s principles. You surface the unspoken friction found in their Risk Disclosures to prove you understand their world.

Fuel Required 10-K / Annual Report (Risk Section) Earnings Call Transcript
"Focus exclusively on the 'Risk Factors' and 'MD&A' sections.

1. Look for mentions of 'legacy friction,' 'failed transformations,' or 'change management delays.'
2. Surface any 'Boring but Expensive' problems (e.g., technical debt costing millions).
3. Draft 3 discovery questions that start with: 'It seems like [ISSUE] is causing...'"
LEVEL 3

Executive Persona Intelligence

Context

People don't buy; personas do. This identifies the Psychological Hook—is your stakeholder a risk-taker or a revenue-protector?.

Fuel Required LinkedIn Profile (About/Posts) Podcast Interviews / YouTube Transcripts
"Analyze the communication style and public statements of [NAME].

1. Determine if their 'Psychological Hook' is Revenue Growth or Revenue Protection.
2. Identify any specific vendor frustrations or 'pet peeves' they have mentioned.
3. Draft a tailored value proposition that uses their specific vocabulary."
LEVEL 3+

The 8-Pillar Enterprise Protocol

The Objective

For deals >£500k. This uncovers the "Management Gap"—the distance between CEO promises and internal Risk Disclosures.

• Foundations & Purpose
• Tech Strategy
• Financial Performance
• Governance & Risk
• Market Segments
• ESG & Sustainability
• Ecosystem & Partners
• Competitive Outlook
Prompt: The Blind Spot Finder
"Perform an 8-Pillar scan on [COMPANY] comparing the CEO’s strategic promises against the 'Risk Factors' in the 10-K.

Identify the **Blind Spot**: Which strategic promise is most at risk due to internal friction? Draft a 'Provocation Statement' I can use to highlight this gap."
THE CLOSER

The Persona Mirror

Context

Transform the AI into a "Digital Twin" of your prospect (e.g., Jennifer Morris) to practice handling objections before the live call.

"You are now [NAME], [TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Your internal mandate is [MANDATE], and you are skeptical of vendors who use jargon. Challenge my assumptions based on your reported 10-K risks and your 'Psychological Hook.'

Respond to this: 'Hi [NAME], I’ve been looking at your Q3 report regarding the 12% unplanned cost increase...'"

Weekly Bonus: The Sinatra Test Filter

Run your drafted emails or decks through this filter to remove "fluff".

"Review my draft. Identify every generic adjective and replace it with a **concrete metric** from the company's 10-K. Ensure every claim follows the Sinatra Test: 'If I can help them solve [SPECIFIC RISK], I can help them solve anything.'"
