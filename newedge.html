The buyer's AI writes the RFP. The vendor's AI writes the response. Every submission is identical. The only remaining edge is human judgment — five disciplines that took 25 years to converge.

The 5 Disciplines (The Human Override)

Sandhurst & Military Strategy

MISSION COMMAND & INTENT

Where your team fails: Sellers freeze when the procurement plan breaks. They wait for manager permission to pivot.

The Edge: Commander's intent embedded. The team knows the strategic outcome. Autonomy to adapt dynamically in the boardroom when the friction changes. No waiting for permission. No freezing when the procurement plan breaks.

Applied Behavioural Psychology

BELIEF MAPPING & SUGGESTIBILITY

Where your team fails: They pitch logic (ROI, features) to a buyer who is secretly terrified of operational failure or losing their job.

The Edge: Map the shadow drivers — Safety, Power, Order. Don't change their mind. Align the solution to their existing subconscious narrative. The buyer feels understood, not sold to.

Harvard & LBS Frameworks

STRUCTURAL NEGOTIATION

Where your team fails: They give away margin at the end of the quarter because they confuse 'haggling' with negotiating.

The Edge: Engineer the deal architecture early. Trade items of low value for items of high value. Lock multi-year terms before procurement engages. Margin protected, not surrendered.

HUMINT (Human Intelligence)

ASSET RECRUITMENT & LEVERAGE

Where your team fails: They map standard org charts and assume the CIO makes the decision. They get blindsided by the CEO's EA.

The Edge: Locate internal champions. Arm them with the exact narrative to defend the solution internally while you're not in the room. The deal advances when you're not there because someone inside is fighting for it.

25 Years of Enterprise Combat

THE EXECUTION GAP BRIDGE

Where your team fails: They mistake AI-generated data for a closing strategy. They have the 'what' but fail the 'how'.

The Edge: Data is not a differentiator. Translating AI outputs into a political, multi-threaded boardroom manoeuvre is the only skill that commands a premium multiple. This cannot be faked or bought — it must be engineered through repetition across hundreds of live deals.

The Knowledge Base (Frameworks)
Expand Foundation Frameworks (01-06) ▼

1. The "So What?" Filter

[G] GATHER

If an insight doesn't directly alter the deal strategy, discard it. Differentiate between 'interesting context' and 'actionable leverage'.

2. The Influence Map

[A] ASSESS

Plotting stakeholders not by title, but by political capital and alignment to the primary friction. Ignore the org chart.

3. Strategic Reframing

[I] INFLUENCE

Shifting the narrative from feature-benefit to risk-mitigation. Changing what the buyer believes they are purchasing.

4. The Annuity Lock

[N] NURTURE

Engineering defensive moats 18 months pre-renewal to ensure the asset is viewed as structural infrastructure, not a vendor.

5. Asymmetric Information

CORE

Gathering and deploying intelligence the incumbent or competitor does not possess, weaponising their blind spots.

6. Friction Identification

RISK

Locating the exact operational or financial pain point driving the buying behaviour, regardless of the stated RFP objective.

Expand Advanced Frameworks (07-12) ▼

7. The Economic Proxy

[I] INFLUENCE

When the true decision-maker is unreachable, recruiting an internal proxy and arming them to fight the political battle on your behalf.

8. Modality Matching

[A] ASSESS

Adjusting presentation styles (visual, auditory, kinesthetic) based on the specific psychological profile of the target executive.

9. The Commodity Bypass

CORE

Refusing to participate in procurement-led feature comparisons by escalating the conversation to board-level risk.

10. The Negative Space

RISK

Analyzing what the client is *not* saying. Using omission in public filings to identify hidden vulnerabilities.

11. Capital Flow Tracking

[G] GATHER

Identifying shifts from OPEX to CAPEX (or vice versa) to structure commercial proposals that align with the CFO's current mandates.

12. The Intelligence Decay

[N] NURTURE

Recognising that account knowledge has a half-life. Systematically refreshing the macro-radar to prevent blind spots post-sale.

The edge is the judgment of what to do with the output.
