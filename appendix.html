The GAIN Doctrine.

The prompts are the code. This is the operating system. The psychological architecture and intelligence protocols that govern the methodology.

PART I: THE HUMAN DRIVERS

Every GAIN prompt is engineered to identify which psychological driver is "starving" in a pursuit. We do not sell features; we satisfy these six needs.

DRIVER 01

SAFETY

Mitigating Fear

The Logic: Buyers are biologically wired to fear vendor failure.

The Protocol: Use the Voss Accusation Audit (Week 1 & 2) to label the fears ("You're probably worried about transition risk"). Naming the fear clears the amygdala response.

DRIVER 02

ORDER

Respecting Time

The Logic: Senior executives equate "noise" with incompetence.

The Protocol: Use the Minto Pyramid Principle (Week 3) to deliver "Answer First." We structure information to reduce cognitive load, signaling that we are a safe pair of hands.

DRIVER 03

POWER

Narrative Lock

The Logic: Executives trust those who speak their specific dialect.

The Protocol: Use Epistemology Mapping (Week 1). We extract the CEO's vocabulary from earnings calls (e.g., "Availability" vs. "Uptime") to mirror their worldview perfectly.

DRIVER 04

ACHIEVEMENT

Legacy Building

The Logic: Stakeholders want to be heroes in their own story.

The Protocol: Use Blue Ocean Logic (Week 3). We shift the deal from "Price Comparison" to "Strategic Leap," aligning the purchase with their promotion path.

DRIVER 05

AFFILIATION

Building Champions

The Logic: Deals are won by internal coalitions when you aren't in the room.

The Protocol: Use Contagious Win Themes (Heath & Heath). We give our champion "Social Currency"â€”narratives so sticky they can easily repeat them to the Board.

DRIVER 06

SUSTAINABILITY

Future Proofing

The Logic: The fear of "Digital Decay" drives churn.

The Protocol: Use Counter-Intelligence (Week 4). We monitor for competitive threats 18 months out, proving we are protecting the client's future, not just their current contract.

PART II: INTELLIGENCE GRADING PROTOCOL

To prevent Intelligence Decay, all synthesis generated by GAIN must be graded. We never treat inference as fact. This is the safety mechanism against AI hallucination.

The Verification Standard

[CONFIRMED]

Verifiable facts from Public Record (Annual Reports, Regulatory Filings) or direct Elicitation (the stakeholder said it). Safe to quote.

[PROBABLE]

Strong behavioral inferences based on role incentives (e.g., "The CFO likely opposes this spend because it hits OpEx"). Use to form hypotheses, not statements.

[SPECULATION]

"Red Flags" or missing data. Requires a "No-Oriented Question" to verify in person. Dangerous if assumed true.

PART III: THE SANDHURST INFLUENCE MODEL

Enterprise sales is a leadership challenge where you have no formal authority over the participants. We apply the British Military doctrine of Mission Command to navigate the deal.

The Sphere of Control

Definition: What you can directly mandate.

In Sales: Your prompts, your research depth, your meeting preparation, your document quality.

The Discipline: Zero tolerance for "Drift" here. This is where Steer, Don't Drift applies.

The Sphere of Influence

Definition: What you must align through belief.

In Sales: Your internal bid team, the client's evaluation panel, the board's decision.

The Discipline: Mission Alignment. You cannot order them to act; you must show how your mission serves their mission (Achievement/Safety). This is how you lead without rank.
