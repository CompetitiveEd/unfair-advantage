THE NARRATIVE

The bid team had worked weekends for three weeks. The response was comprehensive ‚Äî 200 pages of pristine capability data. They scored third.

The winner's response was only 80 pages, but every single section opened with the client's language and directly addressed their pain. [Influence] is the machinery that produces that 80-page response.

THE PSYCHOLOGY (RESONANCE)

Evaluators are tired, bored, and looking for reasons to disqualify you. Cognitive fluency is your greatest weapon. If your response is hard to read or uses generic vendor language, their brain registers it as high-risk. You must reduce their cognitive load by feeding their exact language back to them.

Deal Pre-Mortem Simulator

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Stops optimism bias. Before you write a single word of your proposal, this prompt predicts exactly how you will lose the deal.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

If you can't name the exact reason you will lose, you haven't found the true risk. Hope is not a strategy; preemptive defence is.

MERIDIAN WORKED EXAMPLE

Failure: "We relied on the CIO but the COO blocked us because of transition risk."
Prevention: "Build a zero-disruption transition plan specifically for the COO."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Challenge the AI to focus heavily on the "Gotcha" failure‚Äîthe hidden operational requirement that competitors know about but you don't.

It is 6 months in the future. We have lost the [CLIENT] deal.
The loss review states three reasons:
1. The Political Failure (We backed the wrong person).
2. The Value Failure (We were cheaper but "too risky").
3. The "Gotcha" (We missed a hidden requirement).

Based on the [RFP DOCUMENTS] and [STAKEHOLDER MAP], generate the specific narrative for each of these three failures.
Then, for each failure, give me the ONE action I must take today to prevent it.
[I-02] Internal Data

Win Theme Generator

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Develops 3 'Gold-Standard Win Themes' that solve a specific pain point, are provable with evidence, and are hard for competitors to claim.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

A win theme that only appears in the executive summary is a slogan. A win theme embedded in every single technical and commercial section is a strategy.

MERIDIAN WORKED EXAMPLE

Theme 1: Transformation with Discipline. (Combines innovation with the COO's need for safety).
Theme 2: Commercial Alignment through Gain-Share.

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

If the AI generates a theme like "We are a trusted partner", reject it instantly. Demand themes that are impossible for your competitors to legally claim.

1. It must solve a specific client pain point.
2. It must be provable with evidence we have.
3. It must be hard for competitors to claim.

[I-03] Public Data

Competitor Intelligence Synthesis

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Anticipates the competitor's pitch, what they will whisper about you behind closed doors, and builds your pre-emptive "Kill Shot."

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

You are never bidding in a vacuum. Knowing what they will claim lets you pre-position by making your differentiation obvious without ever naming them.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

Their Ghosting: 'They are too small to scale.'
Kill Shot: 'We guarantee UK-only delivery, unlike global outsourcers who offshore your regulated data.'

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Ask the AI to act as the competitor's Sales Director and write an attack email against your company. It exposes your vulnerabilities instantly.

Act as a Competitive Intelligence Analyst.
We are competing against [COMPETITOR A] and [COMPETITOR B].

Create a 'Competitive Positioning Matrix' (Markdown Table):
1. Their Likely Pitch: What will they claim is their USP?
2. Their Likely Ghosting: What will they whisper about us?
3. Our Counter-Narrative: How do we inoculate the client against their claims?
4. The 'Kill Shot': Where are they demonstrably weak where we are strong?

[I-04] Internal Data

Tier-By-Tier Scoring Strategy

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Ranks criteria by weighted value to tell you exactly where to spend your writing energy to maximize points.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

Junior bid managers try to get 5/5 on everything. Seniors spend 80% of their energy on the 20% of questions that are actually weighted for differentiation.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

High Value (25%): Innovation. Embed 3 case studies.
Low Value (2%): Health & Safety. Provide standard MVR (Minimum Viable Response).

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Force the AI to identify the "Pass/Fail hygiene" questions so you can assign them to junior team members and keep your heavy-hitters focused.

Act as a Bid Manager optimizing for maximum weighted score.
I am providing the Evaluation Criteria and Weightings.

Create a 'Scoring Strategy Table':
1. Rank criteria by 'Weighted Value'.
2. Define exactly what evidence/case studies we must include to score a 5/5 on the top tier.
3. Define the 'Minimum Viable Response' for low-value questions.

Phase 02: Response Writing
[I-05] Internal Data

Executive Summary Generator

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Drafts a 4-Act 'Made to Stick' summary that makes the client's "New Way of Working" the hero, not your company.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

The executive summary is the only section the true decision-makers will actually read. Most are about the bidder. Yours must be about the client.

MERIDIAN WORKED EXAMPLE

The Villain: The 47-minute availability gap.
The Hero: The Meridian 2030 vision.
The Proof: Helios delivered this for Excalibur Financial.

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

The AI will still try to make your product the hero. Edit the output so your product is strictly framed as the "Lightsaber"‚Äîthe tool the client uses to win.

Act as a professional copywriter using the 'Made to Stick' framework.
Draft the structure of the Executive Summary.

Structure it in 4 Acts:
Act 1: The Context (The 'Why Now').
Act 2: The Complication (The 'Villain').
Act 3: The Resolution (The 'Hero'). NOT US. The Hero is the 'New Way of Working'.
Act 4: The Proof (Why Us).

Embed our 3 Win Themes (W3-01) throughout.

Response Section Writer

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Drafts specific response sections that open with the client's challenge, state your approach clearly, and embed the win theme naturally.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

Evaluators skim. If your opening sentence is "We have 20 years of experience," they stop reading. Lead with their pain, end with your proof.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"Question: Confirm ISO 22301.
Answer: We do not hold it today. But our methodology delivered it for Client A. Trust the team that built it."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Use this to draft the "first 80%" of the answer. Then, manually rewrite the opening and closing paragraphs to inject your personal commercial tone.

Act as a senior bid writer producing a response section.

Inputs: 
1. The specific requirement/question.
2. Evaluation criterion.
3. The win theme to embed.
4. Primary evaluator profile.
5. Proof points to include.
6. Word limit.

Write a response that: 
- Opens with THEIR challenge, not your capability. 
- States your approach specifically. 
- Embeds the win theme naturally. 
- Provides evidence for every claim.

[I-07] Internal Data

Pre-Submission Self-Scoring

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Acts as a hostile procurement evaluator to score your draft response strictly against the criteria before submission.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

Internal teams read what they *meant* to write. An AI reads what is *actually* on the page. Use this to find your "Evidence Gaps."

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"Score: 3/5. You answered the question but failed to provide quantitative evidence of past success. To score +1, add a specific metric."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Tell the AI to adopt the persona of your most skeptical stakeholder (e.g., the COO) and review the document solely looking for operational risk.

Act as a hostile procurement evaluator.
I am providing you with the Criteria and our Draft Response.

Task:
1. Score our response out of 5 based STRICTLY on the criteria. Be harsh.
2. Identify: "Where did we answer the question but fail to provide evidence?"
3. Provide 3 specific bullet points on how to move the score up by +1 point.

Phase 03: Execution & Commercials
[I-08] Internal Data

Commercial Narrative Generator

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Writes the strategic narrative wrapper around your pricing schedule, framing it as an investment rather than a cost.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

The strategic framing shapes how evaluators interpret the numbers. Never submit a raw pricing spreadsheet without a narrative attached.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"Framing: Year 1 is an investment year. You are paying for the transformation that lowers Year 3-7 costs by 20%."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Verify every single number the AI references in the narrative against your actual Excel pricing model. Never trust the AI to do math.

Act as a commercial strategist.

Write the narrative wrapper around our pricing (NOT the schedule itself).

Produce:
1. COMMERCIAL PHILOSOPHY: How our model aligns with their stated goals.
2. VALUE FRAMING: Price vs Cost of Inaction / TCO.
3. DIFFERENTIATORS: Gain-share, innovation funds, outcome links.
4. INVESTMENT NARRATIVE: Frame transition costs as investment with payback timeline.
5. OBJECTION PRE-EMPTION: "Why higher than X?"

[I-09] Internal Data

Oral Presentation Strategy

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Scripts the critical first 90 seconds and allocates your speakers to their stakeholders to match credibility perfectly.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

The opening 90 seconds set the tone. Establish you understand their mission better than they do, and they stop evaluating and start listening.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"Speaker Strategy: MD opens to match CIO. Resilience Lead answers technicals. Hook: 'We're here to guarantee your ¬£3.1BN premium flow.'"

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Do not let the AI write a script to read aloud. Use it to build the structural choreography of the room, then speak from your own conviction.

Act as a Presentation Coach for a high-stakes pitch.
Context: We are presenting to [CLIENT].

Task:
1. Script the 'First 90 Seconds': A hook that grabs attention. NOT "Hello, we are Helios."
2. Speaker Allocation Strategy: Match speaker credibility to stakeholder roles.
3. The 'Q&A Prep': Based on Risks (W2-07), what are the 3 hardest questions they will ask?

Engagement Storyboard

Turns your deal strategy into a visual timeline with every touchpoint, governance gate, and submission deadline mapped.

WHERE THIS BREAKS

FAILURE 1: Unprovable Themes

Evaluators loved the executive summary. Then they read the technical response and couldn't find the evidence.

RULE: If you can't point to a specific metric or case study ‚Äî it's not a win theme.

FAILURE 2: Self-Scoring Hubris

Gave ourselves 4.5/5. Actual evaluation came back at 3.2. The internal team knew what they MEANT, not what they WROTE.

RULE: Ask someone who didn't write the response to score it.

FAILURE 3: The Polished Miss

The panel asked one technical question during orals. Our best speaker ‚Äî the sales director ‚Äî froze. The engineer who knew the answer wasn't there.

RULE: The person who answers the hardest question must be in the room.
