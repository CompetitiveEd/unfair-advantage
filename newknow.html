The Objective

Stop showing up to discovery calls asking 'what keeps you up at night?'. The answers are in their public filings. The Gather chain extracts board mandates, financial leaks, executive psychology, competitive positioning, and shadow hierarchies — then synthesises them into a battle card and a narrative lock that gets forwarded to the board. Six prompts. Each one a multi-phase engine with compound intelligence, routing logic, and forensic checklists.

The Psychology

Executives do not buy innovation; they buy the mitigation of operational friction. When you feed AI their exact earnings transcripts, you adopt their internal taxonomy. You stop sounding like a salesperson and start sounding like an operating partner who already understands their P&L.

Governance

Generative AI is a liability if allowed to invent context. The Gather phase restricts LLMs to explicit extraction and synthesis of provided texts. We enforce the "Power of NONE"—if a data point does not exist in the source material, the model must explicitly refuse to generate it.

Execution Checklist

  • Download latest 10-K / Annual Report (PDF).
  • Download latest 2 Earnings Call Transcripts.
  • Create an isolated, zero-retention LLM session.
  • Upload documents as primary context window.
  • Execute G-01 through G-06 sequentially.

Human Checkpoint: Data Integrity

Do not blindly copy/paste the output. Verify the numbers against the source PDF before briefing your team. The LLM is a synthesis engine, but you are the fiduciary of the account strategy.

2. THE CHALLENGE
Economic Impact (G-01 Macro Pain + G-02 OPEX Leak). Board language only.
Use G-02 Named-Process Cost methodology — "The cost of inaction is not directional. It is £X.XM/yr, calculated as [named process × metric × rate]."

3. THE EVIDENCE
Forensic Gap proving current systems are failing. Cite specific data points from G-01/G-02.
If I-04 scoring position is available, weave in: "Independent evaluation against 9 criteria places the current trajectory at [risk level]."

4. THE POSITIONING WEDGE (Dunford — v3 Enhanced)
Position the solution in the strategic category, not the commodity category.
If I-03 competitor intel is available, characterise the competitive landscape without naming: "Scale-first approaches trade proximity for price. Automation-first approaches trade depth for speed. The gap — the category that doesn't yet exist — is [our category]."
This is where the narrative LOCKS. After this paragraph, the reader should think in your category, not the default one.

5. THE OUTCOME
Risk-Adjusted Gain. How reallocating OPEX funds the strategic transformation.
If G-02 Cost Glide Path is available, reference the trajectory: "Year 1 delivers £X in operational efficiency. By Year 3, the cumulative position is £Y."

6. THE RISK (v3 NEW — from I-01 Pre-Mortem)

"The primary execution risk is [X]. The mitigation is [Y]. This is not a risk-free decision — it is a risk-managed one."
This is the Accusation Audit (Voss) at document level — name the fear before they raise it.

THE FORWARD TEST (v3 NEW)
For the primary target executive (from G-03 profile):

Would they forward this exactly as-is? If yes, the document is done.
If not, what would they change? Identify the specific section, the specific concern, and rewrite that section.
Who would they forward it to? Map the forwarding chain. Does the document survive each hop?
THE CORRIDOR TEST (Heath): Would this survive being summarised in a hallway conversation? What is the one sentence that survives? If you can't identify it, the narrative hasn't locked.

Where This Breaks (Failure Modes)

The Hallucination Trap

If you do not strictly enforce the "Power of NONE", the LLM will invent priorities to please you. Pitching a hallucinated pain point destroys credibility in the first five minutes.

The Stale Data Trap

Relying on pre-trained model knowledge instead of uploading the specific PDF. If the model is referencing 2023 data for a 2026 meeting, your strategy is useless.

The Confirmation Bias Trap

Prompting the model to "find reasons why they need [OUR SOLUTION]." You aren't mapping truth; you are demanding an echo chamber. Seek friction, not validation.
