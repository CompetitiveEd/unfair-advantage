The Objective

Stop showing up to discovery calls asking "what keeps you up at night?". The answers are legally mandated to be in their public filings. Gather extracts irrefutable board mandates, specific financial leaks, and macro-strategy shifts so your first interaction is peer-to-peer validation, not vendor interrogation.

The Psychology

Executives do not buy innovation; they buy the mitigation of operational friction. When you feed AI their exact earnings transcripts, you adopt their internal taxonomy. You stop sounding like a salesperson and start sounding like an operating partner who already understands their P&L.

Governance

Generative AI is a liability if allowed to invent context. The Gather phase restricts LLMs to explicit extraction and synthesis of provided texts. We enforce the "Power of NONE"—if a data point does not exist in the source material, the model must explicitly refuse to generate it.

Execution Checklist

  • Download latest 10-K / Annual Report (PDF).
  • Download latest 2 Earnings Call Transcripts.
  • Create an isolated, zero-retention LLM session.
  • Upload documents as primary context window.
  • Execute G-01 through G-06 sequentially.

Human Checkpoint: Data Integrity

Do not blindly copy/paste the output. Verify the numbers against the source PDF before briefing your team. The LLM is a synthesis engine, but you are the fiduciary of the account strategy.

G-01

The Profile (Macro Strategy)

▼

Variables Needed

[COMPANY_NAME]

Architecture / Purpose

Extracts the uncompromising board-level mandates. Establishes the macro-environment context before diving into departmental specifics.

Engine Logic

Forces the model to act as a cynical tier-one consultant, stripping away marketing fluff to find actual operational risk.

Power of "NONE"

Strict constraint: If risks are not explicitly documented in the filings, the model must return "NONE". Prevents hallucinated pain points.

Prompt Code

Act as a Tier 1 management consultant performing a risk and strategy audit on [COMPANY_NAME]. 

Analyze the provided 10-K and earnings call transcripts. Extract the following exactly as stated, prioritizing direct quotes from the CEO/CFO:

1. Top 3 macro-strategic priorities for the next 12-24 months.
2. Top 3 operational or supply chain risks explicitly mentioned as threatening margin.
3. Any mention of digital transformation, technological debt, or infrastructure modernization.

Constraint: You must only use the provided documents. Do not infer or invent. If a category is not explicitly addressed, you must output exactly: "NONE". Format as a clear, bulleted executive summary.
G-02

The Money (Financial Forensics)

▼

Variables Needed

[COMPANY_NAME]

Architecture / Purpose

Locates hard financial leaks and capital allocation shifts. Identifies where money is moving, enabling you to align your solution with active budget streams.

Engine Logic

Bypasses generic revenue numbers to target OPEX vs CAPEX shifts, margin erosion causes, and forward-looking cost reduction mandates.

Expected Output

A quantified list of financial pain points (e.g., "$2.8M loss attributed to legacy IT outages") and CFO directives.

Prompt Code

Based purely on the provided documents for [COMPANY_NAME], execute a forensic financial extraction focusing on margin pressure and capital allocation.

Identify and list:
1. Specific cost-reduction mandates or efficiency targets stated by the CFO.
2. Areas of OPEX expansion or "headwinds" threatening margin.
3. Planned CAPEX investments (specifically related to infrastructure, technology, or transformation).
4. Any mention of vendor consolidation or procurement restructuring.

Constraint: Extract exact figures, percentages, and dollar amounts where available. If a specific point is not discussed, output "NONE". Do not generalize.
G-03

The People (Exec Dossier)

▼

Variables Needed

[ROLE_TITLE] [OUR_SOLUTION]

Override / Human EQ

AI determines title relevance; humans verify political capital. The model maps the stated hierarchy, but you must still validate shadow influence.

Prompt Code

Review the provided texts for mentions of executives, specifically the [ROLE_TITLE] or those managing related functions. We are selling [OUR_SOLUTION].

Map the following:
1. Identify the likely Economic Buyer for our solution based on stated budget ownership in the call.
2. Extract any direct quotes from executives related to the problems our solution solves.
3. Determine the primary KPI or metric that this specific executive is being measured on, based on analyst Q&A responses.

Constraint: If an executive or role is not explicitly mentioned, output "NONE". Do not invent an org chart.
G-04

The Battle (Competitor Intel)

▼

Prompt Code

Scan the provided documents for any mentions of existing technology stacks, strategic vendor partnerships, or incumbent platforms currently deployed by the company.

List:
1. Named vendor partners or platforms.
2. Areas where legacy systems are described as a hindrance, bottleneck, or integration risk.
3. Any stated desire to move from "best-of-breed" to platform consolidation, or vice versa.

Constraint: Only extract explicitly stated technologies or vendor strategies. If none, output "NONE".
G-05

The Match (Value Hypothesis)

▼

Prompt Code

Review the outputs from steps 1 through 4. I am selling [OUR_SOLUTION], which provides [PRIMARY_CAPABILITIES].

Draft three highly specific "Value Hypotheses". Each hypothesis must directly connect one of our capabilities to a specific financial or strategic risk extracted in previous steps.

Format each as:
- The Board Mandate: [Quote or extracted risk]
- The Friction: [Why their current state is failing]
- The Hypothesis: [How our solution de-risks this specific metric]

Do not use marketing jargon. Write in the cynical, risk-averse tone of a CFO.

Where This Breaks (Failure Modes)

The Hallucination Trap

If you do not strictly enforce the "Power of NONE", the LLM will invent priorities to please you. Pitching a hallucinated pain point destroys credibility in the first five minutes.

The Stale Data Trap

Relying on pre-trained model knowledge instead of uploading the specific PDF. If the model is referencing 2023 data for a 2026 meeting, your strategy is useless.

The Confirmation Bias Trap

Prompting the model to "find reasons why they need [OUR SOLUTION]." You aren't mapping truth; you are demanding an echo chamber. Seek friction, not validation.
