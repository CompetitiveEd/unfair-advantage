THE NARRATIVE

James delivered brilliantly for 18 months. SLAs green across the board. Then he lost ¬£2M of expansion business. Not because his delivery was poor, but because while he was focused on what he was already doing, someone else was having a conversation about what the client needed next.

[Nurture] is the system that prevents you from being blindsided in your own account.

THE PSYCHOLOGY (MOMENTUM)

Accounts do not stand still. They are either growing or dying. The moment you transition from "Strategic Partner" to "Reliable Utility" in the client's mind, you have lost control of the account. You must manufacture momentum. The AI's job is to scan the horizon so you can focus on the relationship.

The Warning Brief

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Bridges the gap between Sales and Delivery. Translates the winning proposal into strict commercial boundaries to prevent "scope creep" on Day 1.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

Winning the bid is useless if you lose money on the delivery. This generates the commercial armour that protects your margin.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"The 'No' List: App remediation is strictly excluded. If the client asks for it, it immediately triggers a Change Request."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Do not email this. Put the Delivery Lead in a room, project this output on the screen, and make them verbally confirm they understand the "No" list.

Role: You are a Commercial Risk Officer bridging Sales and Delivery.

Input: Winning Proposal, Commercial Exclusions, Signed Contract.

Task: Draft a "Warning Brief" for the Delivery Lead.
Structure:
1. THE PROMISE: High-level summary of the outcome sold.
2. THE "NO" LIST: Specific exclusions (e.g., "App remediation excluded").
3. THE LANDMINES: Contract vs Proposal differences.
4. COMMERCIAL TRIGGERS: Events that must trigger a Change Request.

Find Expansion Signals

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

The 'Account Edition' of Boring But Expensive. You see their operations from the inside. This finds the NEXT set of problems you can solve.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

Service desk tickets and incident reports are goldmines. Recurring operational friction in areas you *don't* manage is your next pipeline opportunity.

MERIDIAN WORKED EXAMPLE

"Signal: Micro-outage cost is ¬£2.8M and rising. This signals a need for the 'Predictive Resilience' module we didn't sell in Year 1."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Ask your delivery engineers for the raw incident logs, not the polished QBR summary. The AI needs the raw mess to find the patterns.

Act as a Strategic Account Director.

Input: Service reviews, incident reports, meeting minutes.

Task: Identify 'Expansion Signals' (White Space Analysis).
Look for:
1. Operational friction in areas we DON'T manage.
2. Recurring manual workarounds.
3. Complaints about other vendors.
4. Compliance risks adjacent to our scope.

Output as a 'Growth Opportunity Register'.

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Not every opportunity inside an existing account deserves investment. This forces a brutal, evidence-based qualification decision using MEDDIC.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

Account teams often chase "friendly" contacts for dead-end deals while ignoring the hostile stakeholders with actual budget.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"RED: Decision Criteria. (Security Ops may have preferred vendor). Recommendation: PURSUE ‚Äî but fix the red before Month 2."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

If the AI recommends "Pursue" but flags that you don't have access to the Economic Buyer, manually override the decision to "Park."

Act as a Sales Director.

Evaluate opportunity against MEDDIC framework. Score each letter Red/Amber/Green.
- Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion.

Recommendation: PURSUE, PARK, or WALK AWAY? Explain why.

New Leadership Response Protocol

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

A rapid-response plan for the first 30 days when a new executive joins your client. Every new leader is both a threat and an opportunity.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

A meeting without intelligence is a pitch. A meeting with intelligence is a conversation. Get informed first, get introduced second.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"Threat: New CIO used our main competitor at their last firm. Plan: Do not pitch. Lead with a 'State of the Union' data briefing on their new estate."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

If the AI suggests reaching out directly on LinkedIn, ignore it. Have your executive sponsor at the client introduce you as a trusted advisor.

A new senior leader has joined the client organization.

1. PROFILE: Career, priorities, previous vendors.
2. THREAT ASSESSMENT: Did they use a competitor?
3. OPPORTUNITY ASSESSMENT: Alignment with our unsold capabilities?
4. 30-DAY ENGAGEMENT PLAN: Who introduces us? What do we lead with?
5. FIRST MEETING BRIEFING.

Threat Detection Scan

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Analyses subtle signals to detect if a competitor is circling your account before it's too late.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

Competitors don't announce they are targeting your account. They do it through LinkedIn connections, conference alignments, and quiet meetings.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"Threat (HIGH): Procurement lead connected with two competitor Sales Directors last week. Recommend: Pre-emptive commercial health-check meeting."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Don't act paranoid. If you detect a threat, never mention the competitor. Just aggressively reinforce your unique value in that exact area.

Act as a competitive intelligence analyst.

Check for signals: LinkedIn connections, Hiring patterns, Event attendance, Operational mentions, Procurement activity.

For each signal: Rate threat level (LOW/MED/HIGH) and recommend action.

Value Realisation Report

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Translates operational metrics into a board-level report focused on business outcomes enabled by the partnership.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

SLA reports are for IT managers. Value reports are for the board. If the board only sees how much you cost, and never sees what you enable, you are a commodity.

MERIDIAN WORKED EXAMPLE

"SLA: 99.7% availability.
Value Report: 'Reduced alert noise by 67%, freeing 340 FTE hours for the Meridian 2030 programme.'"

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

If the AI cannot tie your operational metric to one of the CFO's strategic pillars (found in Phase G), then the metric is useless to the board.

Act as a strategic account director producing a board-level report.

Produce:
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: Business value delivered.
2. OUTCOMES DELIVERED: "Protected ¬£X revenue" not "Resolved incident".
3. VALUE BEYOND CONTRACT: Innovation, risk mitigation.
4. STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT: Mapping to their priorities.
5. FORWARD VIEW.

Continuous Intelligence Monitor

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Runs a monthly automated check of your original Gather intelligence to flag exactly what has changed.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

The original intelligence you gathered in Month 1 is obsolete by Month 6. If you don't refresh the data, you'll pitch an old strategy to a new problem.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"Change Flag: Meridian posted 3 new job listings for 'Cloud Security Architect' ‚Äî signals Phase 2 of transformation is moving to execution."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Set a calendar reminder for the first Tuesday of every month. Feed the new PRs and Job Board data into this prompt. It takes 5 minutes to stay relevant.

Act as an intelligence analyst.

Compare this month's sources against baseline. Flag ONLY changes:
1. NEW STRATEGIC SIGNALS.
2. LEADERSHIP CHANGES.
3. FINANCIAL CHANGES.
4. REGULATORY CHANGES.
5. COMPETITIVE SIGNALS.

Internal Business Case Builder

[+ VIEW]
THE OBJECTIVE

Builds the commercial case for internal investment. Before you can win the deal externally, you need to win the resource allocation internally.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

If your internal business case doesn't include an honest assessment of "Win Probability," your leadership is making resource decisions blind.

OUTPUT EXAMPLE

"Investment Required: 6 weeks of bid team time. Return: ¬£65M contract, 14% margin, Flagship Reference. Recommendation: Pursue."

THE HUMAN OVERRIDE

Do not let the AI fabricate margin projections. Inject your exact delivery costs manually before generating the final report.

Act as a pursuit director. Build a one-page investment case.

1. OPPORTUNITY SUMMARY.
2. WIN PROBABILITY (Honest assessment).
3. INVESTMENT REQUIRED (Hours, cost).
4. EXPECTED RETURN.
5. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE.
6. RISK FACTORS.
7. RECOMMENDATION (Pursue/Park/Walk).

WHERE THIS BREAKS

FAILURE 1: The Silent Exit

The dashboard was green. The AI sentiment analysis said "Strong". But the key decision-maker stopped attending QBRs three months ago. We lost the renewal.

RULE: Data is not a relationship. If you stop reading the room, you lose.

FAILURE 2: The Fiction Campaign

Built a beautiful 90-day campaign. Reviewed it once. Never looked at it again. By the time the RFP arrived, the campaign was entirely fiction.

RULE: A campaign without weekly governance is just a document.

FAILURE 3: Delivery Entitlement

Delivery was excellent for four years. But when the renewal started, procurement had already shortlisted competitors. Someone else was selling while we were resting.

RULE: Delivery earns the right to renew. It doesn't guarantee it. Run the Threat Scan continuously.
