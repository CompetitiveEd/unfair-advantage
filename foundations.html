Compliance & Data Protection

⚠️ What's Safe to Upload

✅ SAFE:

  • Published annual reports
  • Press releases
  • Public LinkedIn profiles
  • News articles

❌ NOT SAFE:

  • CRM data
  • Proposals with pricing
  • Internal documents
  • Confidential customer info

Rule: If you wouldn't post it publicly on LinkedIn, don't upload it to a free AI tool.

Two-AI Verification Rule

For critical intelligence, verify with two different AI tools.

You: "How many times does 'cost-to-serve' appear?"

Claude: 17 times
ChatGPT: 17 times
→ HIGH confidence ✅

Claude: 17 times
Gemini: 3 times
→ LOW confidence ⚠️ Verify manually

Why this works:

  • Different AI models process differently
  • If both agree, likely accurate
  • If they disagree, manual check required

Use this for:

  • Financial data
  • Quote verification
  • Language pattern frequency
  • Executive names and roles

Level 3+: The 8-Pillar Enterprise Protocol

⚠️ Use ONLY For High-Stakes Deals

  • ✅ Deal size >£500K
  • ✅ Board-level presentation
  • ✅ Multi-year partnership
  • ✅ 90-120 minutes available

DO NOT use for: Quick meeting prep, deals <£100K, routine prospecting

For 80% of your meetings, Level 1 (20 minutes) is sufficient. But for the final 5%—the £500K+ transformational deals—this protocol provides institutional-grade intelligence.

Two-Step Framework

STEP 1: Intake & Calibration

This calibrates the AI to your specific industry and target.

Act as a Senior Market Intelligence Lead. Your first task is to ask me for a Target Company Name.

Once I provide the name, you must:

1. Define the Industry Context: Identify the primary sector (e.g., Construction, Fintech, Pharma, SaaS).

2. Select Validation Sources: Name 3-4 high-authority sources for that sector.
   
   Examples by sector:
   - Construction: Construction Enquirer, PBCToday, Construction News
   - Finance: Bloomberg, FT, SEC Filings, Annual Reports
   - Technology: Gartner, IDC, company investor relations
   - Pharma: FDA filings, PubMed, industry journals

3. Identify Key Competitors: List 3 direct rivals to use as a baseline for competitive analysis.

Wait for my confirmation before executing the 8-point research.

STEP 2: 8-Pillar Execution

After confirmation from Step 1, use this prompt:

Execute a Deep Research Protocol for [INSERT COMPANY NAME] across the following 8 areas.

CRITICAL REQUIREMENT: Use a Double-Verification Loop—cross-reference at least TWO high-authority sources for every financial or strategic claim. Flag any single-source claims explicitly.

THE 8 PILLARS OF INVESTIGATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. FOUNDATIONS
   - Establishment date, headquarters location, ownership structure
   - Core business model and value proposition
   - Mission, values, and "Cultural DNA" (what they say about themselves)
   - Leadership history (founders, CEO tenure, major transitions)

2. FINANCIALS (2022-2025/TTM)
   - Annual revenue (trend analysis)
   - Net income, operating income, PBT (if public)
   - Available financial ratios (margins, growth rates)
   - NOTE: If private company, reconstruct from:
     * Annual Reviews
     * Companies House filings (UK)
     * Reputable trade news estimates
     * Explicitly flag "ESTIMATED" vs "REPORTED"

3. MARKET SEGMENTS & SERVICES
   - Primary business segments (by revenue %)
   - Specific services/products in each segment
   - Recent product/service launches or expansions
   - Competitive advantages vs. identified rivals
   - Market positioning (premium, value, niche specialist)

4. TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY
   - AI/ML/Automation focus areas
   - Data analytics capabilities and platforms
   - R&D investment levels (% of revenue if available)
   - Innovation labs or emerging tech initiatives
   - Shift toward "Agentic AI" or autonomous systems (if applicable)

5. GOVERNANCE & LEADERSHIP
   - Board of Directors composition
   - Executive team (C-suite)
   - Key leaders in: Technology, Operations, Digital Transformation
   - Recent leadership changes or appointments
   - Advisory boards or strategic councils

6. INFRASTRUCTURE & ESG
   - Core technology systems and platforms
   - Cybersecurity posture (certifications, incidents)
   - Net Zero commitments and progress
   - Sustainability initiatives
   - How technology enables ESG goals

7. ECOSYSTEM & PARTNERSHIPS
   - Geographic presence (countries, offices, headcount)
   - Key client segments (industries served, client types)
   - Technology partnerships (Microsoft, AWS, Oracle, SAP, etc.)
   - Strategic alliances or joint ventures
   - M&A activity (acquisitions, divestitures)

8. SYNTHESIS & COMPETITIVE OUTLOOK
   - Industry rankings (Fortune 500, sector awards, certifications)
   - Analyst ratings or recognitions (Gartner, Forrester, etc.)
   - Current "Competitive Moat" (sustainable advantages)
   - Major challenges or threats (2026 outlook)
   - Strategic priorities for next 12-24 months

BONUS SECTION: BLIND SPOT IDENTIFICATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Identify ONE significant event or dynamic NOT covered in the 8 pillars:
- Major M&A announcement
- Leadership crisis or executive departure
- Regulatory challenge or legal issue
- Structural carve-out or reorganization
- Competitive disruption or market shift

FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

For each pillar:
- Use bulleted lists (3-5 main points per pillar)
- Include 2-3 sub-bullets for deep data under each main point
- Cite sources explicitly: [Source: Annual Report 2024, p.12]
- Flag "Data Gaps" for private entities or unavailable information
- Mark "ESTIMATED" vs "REPORTED" for all financial data
- Highlight contradictions between sources

Why This Protocol Works

  • Double-Verification Loop: Forces AI to cross-reference sources, reduces hallucination risk
  • Industry-Specific Calibration: Step 1 prevents generic research, AI selects appropriate authoritative sources
  • Structured 8-Pillar Framework: Ensures comprehensive coverage, nothing gets missed
  • Explicit Gap Identification: AI flags what it DOESN'T know, "Estimated" vs "Reported" transparency
  • Blind Spot Detection: Forces AI to look for non-obvious dynamics, recent events or crises

The Reality Check

Most sellers will never use this. And that's fine.

For 80% of meetings, Level 1 (20 minutes) is enough.

For 15%, Level 2 (45 minutes) adds depth.

For the final 5%—the £500K+ transformational deals—this protocol gives you institutional-grade intelligence that competitors using "quick Google searches" simply can't match.

Use it when the stakes justify the time.

Time Required: 90-120 minutes minimum (complex deals with extensive verification can extend to 2-3 hours - if verification takes longer, that's not a bug, it's due diligence)
