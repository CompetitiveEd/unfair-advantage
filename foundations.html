The Story: How Research Won a Major Deal

Tom, a veteran AE, recently lost a deal because the competitor "seemed to understand the business better". They had used the exact language the executive team was using internally.

For his next pitch, Tom spent 20 minutes with AI analyzing annual reports and earnings calls. He found "cost-to-serve" mentioned 17 times, while "innovation" appeared only twice.

Outcome: Tom repositioned from "digital transformation" to "operational efficiency with managed risk" and won a multi-year contract.

Honesty Check: When AI Gets It Wrong

Maintaining executive credibility requires knowing the failure modes:

  • Hallucinations: If AI can't cite a page number or quote, it doesn't exist.
  • Context Collapse: AI may cite an executive who left 18 months ago; always verify with LinkedIn.
  • The Over-Reliance Trap: Generic AI questions yield generic "everyone asks that" responses.
