The Foundations

The Commercial Operating System: Why Judgement is the Moat in an AI-Driven World.

The 2026 Market Pattern

Marc Sumner (Robertson Sumner) identified the rarest and most in-demand roles as those blending technical literacy with commercial acumen and soft skills. In this environment, AI fluency is assumed; output and judgement are the differentiators.

The Shift: From Managing Teams to Orchestrating Workflows

  • Hiring Trends: Expectations are rising faster than job descriptions.
  • The McKinsey Rule: Elite firms now screen for those who can "maintain ownership of decisions when AI outputs are imperfect".
  • Human-Agent Workflows: Leadership has shifted from managing human teams to orchestrating human-agent workflows.

The Story: How Research Won a Major Deal

Tom, a veteran AE, recently lost a deal because the competitor "seemed to understand the business better". They used the exact language the executive team was using internally.

For his next pitch, Tom spent 20 minutes with AI analyzing annual reports and earnings calls. He found "cost-to-serve" mentioned 17 times, while "innovation" appeared only twice.

Outcome: Tom repositioned from "digital transformation" to "operational efficiency with managed risk" and won a multi-year contract.

The Intellectual Bedrock

Insight-Led Selling

• Timme & Astley

The Principle: Adopt an Executive Mindset. Shift from "what am I selling?" to "what are THEY trying to solve?". Use their vocabulary, not vendor jargon.

Blue Sky Thinking

• Second-Order Thinking

The Principle: Look past the surface. When a prospect says "digital transformation," they often mean "help us manage risk". Identify connections and ripple effects beyond the immediate transaction.

Human Amplification Index™

• Sol Rashidi

The Principle: AI handles the Dull (data scanning) and the Difficult (cross-referencing) so you can focus on the Distinct (strategic repositioning and emotional intelligence).

The Sinatra Test

• Heath Brothers (SUCCESs)

The Principle: Concrete and Credible messaging. Use the CFO's exact data points (e.g., "every 1% improvement in response efficiency = £2-3M in avoided losses") to prove you understand their hardest challenges.

Tactical Empathy

• Chris Voss

The Principle: The "Accusation Audit". Proactively surface their objections or past failures (like communication gaps during recovery) before they do to prove you understand their world.

Honesty Check: When AI Gets It Wrong

Maintaining executive credibility requires knowing the failure modes:

  • Hallucinations: If AI can't cite a page number or quote, it doesn't exist.
  • Context Collapse: AI may cite an executive who left 18 months ago; always verify with LinkedIn.
  • The Over-Reliance Trap: Generic AI questions yield generic "everyone asks that" responses.
