The Intellectual Bedrock

Insight-Led Selling

• Timme & Astley

The Principle: Adopt an Executive Mindset. Shift from "what am I selling?" to "what are THEY trying to solve?". Use their vocabulary, not vendor jargon.

Blue Sky Thinking

• Second-Order Thinking

The Principle: Look past the surface. When a prospect says "digital transformation," they often mean "help us manage risk". Identify connections and ripple effects beyond the immediate transaction.

Human Amplification Index™

• Sol Rashidi

The Principle: AI handles the Dull (data scanning) and the Difficult (cross-referencing) so you can focus on the Distinct (strategic repositioning and emotional intelligence).

Made to Stick

• Heath Brothers (SUCCESs)

The Principle: Concrete and Credible messaging. Use the CFO's exact data points (e.g., "cost-to-serve mentioned 17 times") to prove you understand their hardest challenges.

Tactical Empathy

• Chris Voss

The Principle: The "Accusation Audit". Proactively surface their objections or past project failures to prove you understand their world before asking for a commitment.

Honesty Check: When AI Gets It Wrong

Maintaining executive credibility requires knowing the failure modes:

  • Hallucinations: If AI can't cite a page number or quote, it doesn't exist.
  • Context Collapse: AI may cite an executive who left 18 months ago; always verify with LinkedIn.
  • The Over-Reliance Trap: Generic AI questions yield generic "everyone asks that" responses.
