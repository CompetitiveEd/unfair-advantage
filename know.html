20 MINUTES — FROM ANNUAL REPORT TO EXECUTIVE BRIEFING
THE PSYCHOLOGY: DRIVER 01 (SAFETY)

Research is not about gathering facts. It is about lowering buyer cortisol. Humans are biologically wired to fear vendor failure. When you ask basic discovery questions ("Tell me about your priorities"), you trigger that fear. When you quote their own earnings call back to them to frame your solution, you prove you are a safe pair of hands. This core prompt chain ensures you never walk into a room blind.

The Prompt Chain

COMPANY INTEL → FIND MONEY → PROFILE PEOPLE → BATTLE CARD → CAPABILITY → BRIEFING

Enterprise sales is a chessboard, not a straight line. Steps 1-3 extract intelligence. Steps 4-5 synthesise strategy. Step 6 creates the deliverable.

[G-01] Source Scraping Protocol

Public Data Only
Before running any intelligence prompt, you need raw material. Paste this prompt into your AI to establish the context window, then feed it the documents. 
Before starting the GAIN intelligence chain, I am going to feed you raw source material. Do not analyse it yet. Just confirm receipt.

I will provide:
1. Company website: About Us, Services, Leadership
2. Annual report / investor presentation text
3. LinkedIn company page: Recent posts, open jobs
4. Target stakeholder LinkedIn profiles
5. Any available press releases or news coverage

Acknowledge this instruction. I will paste the data in the next prompt.
WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

The "Careers" page is the most underused intelligence source. If a company says they are focused on AI, but has zero open reqs for Data Scientists, the initiative is fake. If they have 14 open roles for compliance officers, the regulator has them under pressure.

[G-02] 9-Pillar Deep Research

Public Data Only
Extracts the intelligence architecture of the company. Returns board-level priorities, budget signals, and pain points structured specifically for enterprise sales. 
Act as a Senior Market Intelligence Analyst preparing a pre-meeting briefing for an enterprise sales executive.

Using the source data provided, extract intelligence across these 9 pillars:

1. STRATEGIC PRIORITIES: Board-level goals and CEO stated direction. Quote their exact language.
2. FINANCIAL SIGNALS: Revenue trends, margin pressure, investment areas. Flag specific numbers.
3. PAIN POINTS: Stated challenges, risk disclosures, failed initiatives. Note what's buried vs. what's highlighted.
4. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING: How they describe their market position. Who do they benchmark against?
5. TECHNOLOGY DIRECTION: Digital transformation priorities, platform investments.
6. REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE: Upcoming deadlines, certification targets, audit findings.
7. PEOPLE & CULTURE: Hiring priorities, skills gaps, leadership changes.
8. STAKEHOLDER LANGUAGE: The specific phrases executives use repeatedly.
9. TIMELINE & DEADLINES: Fiscal year, budget cycles, programme milestones.

FORMAT: Structure each pillar with 3-5 key findings. For every claim, cite the source document. Flag anything you cannot verify as "UNVERIFIED."
WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

Pay attention to Pillar 8 (Stakeholder Language). If the CEO says "Continuous Business Availability" and you pitch "Disaster Recovery," you sound like a generic vendor. If you use their exact language, you sound like an insider.

[G-03] Budget Signal Detection

Public Data Only
Amateurs pitch "invest more." Professionals find money the prospect is already wasting. This prompt finds the "Boring But Expensive" reallocation budget. 
Using the company research, identify "Boring But Expensive" problems — operational costs that are high, visible to the CFO, and solvable but unglamorous.

Look for these categories:
1. MANUAL PROCESSES: Workflows requiring human intervention that could be automated.
2. INTEGRATION FRICTION: Data reconciliation between systems, manual handoffs.
3. COMPLIANCE BURDEN: Audit preparation labour, certification maintenance.
4. VENDOR DEPENDENCY: Over-reliance creating premium pricing or switching costs.
5. TECHNICAL DEBT: End-of-life systems, extended support contracts.

For each finding:
— Estimate the pain/cost where data supports it.
— Identify who owns this budget (which function).
— Frame the reallocation argument: "You're currently spending time/money on [boring problem]. Redirecting even a portion toward [solution] eliminates the root cause."
WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

If a company mentions a shift from "self-assessment" to "structured vendor interviews" in their risk disclosures, that implies massive manual labour cost. That is your entry point. Solve the boring problem first.

[G-04] Executive Belief Decoder

Public Data Only
Builds a profile beyond job titles. Surfaces their decision-making style and the "One Question" that proves you understand their personal agenda. 
Analyse the LinkedIn profile, recent posts, and public commentary provided for [NAME], [TITLE].

I don't want to know what this person does. I want to know how they decide.

Analyse:
1. STATED PRIORITIES: What themes dominate their public commentary? 
2. DECISION-MAKING STYLE: Evidence-based or intuitive? Collaborative or directive? Risk-averse or progressive? Cite specific examples from their content.
3. PROFESSIONAL REPUTATION: How do colleagues describe them in endorsements? 
4. THE DIAGNOSTIC QUESTION: Based on their communication style, what will they test me on? What question will they ask in the first 10 minutes to judge my credibility?
5. THE ONE QUESTION: Give me ONE discovery question to ask them that demonstrates I understand their personal agenda. It must make them think: "This person has done their homework."
WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

If an executive frequently shares their team's failures publicly (e.g., "We missed our Q3 deployment target, here is why..."), they value transparency over polish. Do not walk into that meeting with a glossy, perfect slide deck. Lead with what your solution struggles with.

HUMAN CHECKPOINT (EQ OVERRIDE)

Do not run the final briefing prompt yet. Stop and read the AI's analysis. Does the tone feel right for this specific executive? Are the budget arguments realistic for this sector? The AI has assembled the data—you must now inject your commercial intuition. Filter out the noise before generating the final asset.

[G-05] Synthesise Battle Card

Public Data Only
Turns intelligence into ammunition. A single-page reference for the meeting: Hooks, Landmines, and the "North Star". 
Using all the intelligence gathered so far — company research, budget signals, and stakeholder profile — create a 1-page Executive Battle Card.

Structure:

1. CFO NORTH STAR: The single strategic priority that defines this company's direction right now. Use their exact language.
2. THE THREE HOOKS: Three conversation-opening insights derived from the research. Each hook must:
   — Reference a specific finding from the research (with source)
   — Connect to a capability you could provide
   — Be framed as an observation, not a pitch ("I noticed..." not "We offer...")
3. BOARDROOM QUESTION: One question designed for a board-level audience that demonstrates strategic understanding of their roadmap.
4. LANDMINES: What topics should you AVOID? What past failures or sensitivities should you navigate carefully?
WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

The Landmine section is critical. If the prospect recently fired a vendor for a botched cloud migration, talking about your own cloud capabilities too early will trigger their defense mechanisms. You must navigate around the blast radius.

[G-06] Capability Matching

Internal Data Risk
Don't pitch generic capabilities. Match your proof points to their specific pain points and language. WARNING: Requires pasting internal company materials into the AI. 
Based on the company research, stakeholder profile, and battle card I've provided, search the following materials from MY company for the best matches:

[PASTE: Your company's case studies, solution descriptions, or capability statements]

Find:
1. CASE STUDIES: Which client stories are most relevant to this prospect's specific situation? Match on industry, challenge type, scale, and outcomes.
2. PROOF POINTS: Specific metrics or outcomes from our work that directly address their stated pain points.
3. LANGUAGE ALIGNMENT: Where do our materials already use their language? Where do we need to translate? 
4. GAPS: Where does this prospect need something we don't have a clear proof point for? These become discovery questions, not weaknesses to hide.
WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

You aren't looking for a perfect product match; you are looking for a story. If the client is struggling with manual integration overhead, pull the case study where you eliminated 40 hours of manual reconciliation, not the one where you built a shiny new app.

[G-07] The Executive Briefing

Public Data Only
The output isn't a 20-page research doc. It's a scannable briefing designed to be read on a phone screen 10 minutes before you walk into the room. 
Using all the human-verified intelligence from the previous steps, compile a final Executive Briefing for my meeting with [NAME].

Structure the briefing as follows:

SECTION 1: THE CONTEXT
Strategic direction in 3 sentences using their language. Key pain points with severity ranking. Hard deadlines driving urgency.

SECTION 2: MEETING OBJECTIVES
Primary objective: The one outcome that makes this meeting a success.
Discovery questions: The 3 exact questions I must ask, ranked by priority.

SECTION 3: KEY MESSAGING (THE HOOKS)
Three conversation-opening insights derived from the research. 

SECTION 4: LANDMINES
What topics should I AVOID? What past failures or sensitivities should I navigate carefully?

FORMAT: Concise. Bullet points. Ready to read on a mobile device.
WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

The briefing isn't a script to read aloud; it's a confidence engine. When you know the 'Landmines' and 'Hooks' cold, your cortisol drops. You stop thinking about your notes and start actively listening to the room. That presence is what wins the deal.

ADVANCED INTELLIGENCE ARCHIVE // For Practitioner Use

The core pipeline above solves 80% of meetings. These specialised prompts are for the other 20%: complex accounts, highly regulated entities, and deep-dive deal forensics. Click to copy directly to your clipboard.

W1-01
Before starting the GAIN intelligence chain, I am going to feed you raw source material. Do not analyse it yet. Just confirm receipt. I will provide: Company website, Annual report, LinkedIn company page, Target stakeholder profiles.
W1-02
Act as a Market Intelligence Analyst preparing a rapid pre-meeting brief. Extract intelligence across the 9 pillars (Strategy, Finance, Pain Points, Competitors, Tech, Regs, People, Language, Timeline) but in COMPACT format. FORMAT: Bullet points. Maximum 3 per pillar. No pillar should exceed 4 lines. Flag anything unverified. This briefing should be readable in 3 minutes.
W1-04
Based on the company's fiscal year end (FYE), predict their budget cycle behaviour. 1. IDENTIFY: When is their FYE? 2. MAPPING: Budget Setting Phase (Months 10-12)? Release Phase (Months 1-2)? Use-it-or-lose-it Phase (Month 11)? 3. CURRENT STATUS: Where are we TODAY in their cycle? 4. STRATEGY: Should I be pitching "spend now for impact" or "plan now for next year's budget"?
W1-06
Analyse this LinkedIn profile. Produce a COMPACT PROFILE: 1. ROLE SUMMARY: What they own. Who they report to. One sentence. 2. TOP 3 PRIORITIES: Based on posts. Three bullet points. 3. DECISION STYLE: Evidence-based or intuitive? One sentence. 4. THE ONE QUESTION: A discovery question proving deep homework. 5. RAPPORT HOOK: Personal dimension for an opener.
W1-07
Analyse these 3 recent job descriptions to find the "Hidden Mandate." 1. WHAT'S MISSING: What skills are they hiring for that they don't have internally? 2. TECH STACK: What specific tools are listed? 3. REPORTING LINE: Who does this role report to? 4. KEYWORDS: What words appear most often? 5. CONCLUSION: Based on these hires, what unannounced project are they about to launch?
W1-10
Based on the intelligence provided, audit my standard presentation deck outline. For each slide, recommend: KEEP, CUT, MODIFY, REORDER, or CREATE. CRITICAL: The first slide after the title must address THEIR context, not our company overview. Limit to 12 slides for a 30-min meeting.
W1-11
Imagine it is 6 months from now and we LOST this deal. Generate the 5 most likely reasons why we lost (e.g., Status Quo, Price, Stalled, Ignored Pain Point, Blocked by Detractor). For each reason, give me one pre-emptive action to take NOW to prevent it.
W1-12
Based on the company research, stakeholder profile, and battle card — identify the 3 most likely objections this prospect will raise. Write an Accusation Audit (Voss framework) for each. Format: "You're probably thinking [specific concern]. And you'd be right to think that. Here's what I'd want to know if I were in your position..."
W1-13
Verify every specific claim in the generated briefing against the original source text. Output a table: 1. CLAIM | 2. SOURCE (exact quote) | 3. VERDICT (VERIFIED / AMBIGUOUS / HALLUCINATED). If you cannot find a direct quote, mark it HALLUCINATED.
W1-15
CROSS-REFERENCE the provided sources (Website vs LinkedIn vs Financials). 1. NARRATIVE GAPS: Where does the website say one thing and LinkedIn/Financials say another? 2. URGENCY SIGNALS: Build a timeline of recent announcements to explain WHY NOW. 3. HIDDEN INTEL: What appears on only one source that others omit?
W1-16
Map the Governance structure and Non-Executive Directors (NEDs). 1. COMMITTEE STRUCTURE: Who chairs Audit, Risk, and Remuneration? Which committee approves this deal? 2. NED NETWORK MAP: What other boards do these NEDs sit on? Are there shared NEDs with our existing clients (for warm intros)?

Intelligence gathered. Now, map the politics.
