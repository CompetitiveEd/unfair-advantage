The Narrative

Stop answering questions like a student taking a test. Influence means taking control of the evaluation criteria. We don't respond to RFPs; we reframe them to highlight risks the incumbent cannot solve.

The Psychology

Boards read executive summaries; evaluators read appendices. The proposal must satisfy the paranoid CFO hunting for cost certainty, and the pedantic engineer looking for technical depth, simultaneously.

Data Governance Legend

Never upload proprietary pricing models or unanonymised client data into open LLMs. Enforce strict constraints to prevent AI hallucinating features or SLA guarantees you cannot legally fulfill.

Execution Ingredients

  • RFP/RFI documents & explicit evaluation criteria.
  • Your existing proposal library and boilerplate.
  • [G] Gather phase macroeconomic insights.
  • [A] Assess phase shadow map (detractors/buyers).

Human Checkpoint: OPSEC

Never feed unredacted client financial IP or unreleased pricing models into public LLMs. Sanitise inputs to zero-trust standards before execution.

Human Checkpoint: Proprietary Multiplier

AI writes generic "thought leadership." You must manually inject your firm's unique asymmetric insights to shatter the commodity trap and win.

I-01

The Exec Summary Reframer

▼

Variables Needed

[RFP_EXECUTIVE_SUMMARY] [G_MACRO_RADAR]

Architecture / Purpose

Rewrites the standard RFP intro into a narrative aligned with actual board-level friction, anchoring the entire submission around CEO/CFO pain points.

Engine Logic

Strips generic vendor platitudes. Replaces "we are thrilled to respond" with hard risk mitigation metrics and financial certainty.

Power of "NONE"

If no board-level risk exists in the inputs, the engine must not invent one. It must output "NONE" to force a human review.

Prompt Code

Act as an elite bid director communicating with a Fortune 500 board. Rewrite this [RFP_EXECUTIVE_SUMMARY] using the intelligence extracted in [G_MACRO_RADAR].

Objective: Shift the narrative from "fulfilling technical requirements" to "mitigating the board's stated financial and operational risks."
Constraint: Do not use the phrases "thrilled to partner," "best in class," or "synergy." Use assertive, risk-averse language. If the [G_MACRO_RADAR] lacks explicit risk data, output "NONE" and halt.
I-02

The Value Prop Aligner

▼

Variables Needed

[OUR_STANDARD_VALUE_PROP] [A_ECONOMIC_BUYER]

Architecture / Purpose

Maps your generic product benefits strictly to the KPIs and stated objectives of the true Economic Buyer.

Engine Logic

Ruthlessly deletes any feature that does not directly impact the buyer's stated bonus criteria, risk profile, or operational mandate.

Governance Constraint

Use exact metrics only. No generalized ROI claims. The AI must discard unprovable marketing numbers.

Prompt Code

Review our [OUR_STANDARD_VALUE_PROP] against the specific KPIs and mandates of the [A_ECONOMIC_BUYER].

Task: Delete any feature or benefit that does not directly serve this specific executive's goals. Rewrite the remaining benefits as direct solutions to their stated problems. 
Constraint: Quantify outcomes using only numbers that exist in the provided source data. If a specific linkage cannot be made, discard the feature.

The Detractor Neutraliser

▼

Variables Needed

[A_DETRACTOR_PROFILE] [OUR_WEAKNESSES]

Architecture / Purpose

Pre-empts the internal objections from the specific hostile stakeholders identified during the Assess phase.

Engine Logic

Constructs specific 'landmines' for the incumbent/competitor and defensive moats for our own functional weaknesses.

Output Expected

A bulleted list of defensive statements ready to insert into the proposal methodology section.

Prompt Code

Analyze the [A_DETRACTOR_PROFILE] and map it against [OUR_WEAKNESSES]. 

Draft a preemptive defense narrative that we can embed into the proposal. The goal is to answer the detractor's likely objections regarding our deployment timeline and technical gaps before they raise them to the committee. 
Frame our weakness as a deliberate, strategic choice designed to ensure stability and reduce migration risk.

The Ghost Writer (SME Extraction)

▼

Variables Needed

[SME_ROUGH_NOTES] [RFP_QUESTION]

Architecture / Purpose

Converts bullet points or raw transcripts from busy technical engineers into polished, RFP-compliant narrative instantly.

Engine Logic

Scales up technical shorthand into formal, persuasive text while fiercely protecting the deep technical accuracy.

Override / Human EQ

You must review the output with the SME. The AI will make it sound professional, but the engineer must verify it remains physically possible.

Prompt Code

Take the [SME_ROUGH_NOTES] provided by our lead architect and use them to answer [RFP_QUESTION].

Format the response logically for a technical evaluator. Expand on shorthand where appropriate, but strictly maintain the technical boundaries. Do not invent capabilities we do not possess. Match the tone of a senior, authoritative engineer.

Where This Breaks (Failure Modes)

The RFP Trap

Answering exactly what procurement asked, exactly how they asked it. Doing this effectively commoditises your solution instantly.

The Feature Dump

Using AI to effortlessly generate 10 pages of technical specifications the board will never read, burying your business case in noise.

The Ghost Writer

Relying on AI to write the entire bid end-to-end without SME injection. It sounds grammatically perfect, but says absolutely nothing of substance.
