3. PROCUREMENT & SUPPLY CHAIN CAPABILITY
================================================================

--- Our Model: Services-Led, Procurement-Enabled ---

Helios is not a VAR. We don't make margin on shifting boxes.
But we learned early — the hard way — that delivering
managed services without controlling the supply chain creates
gaps that hurt clients.

The lesson came from Cavendish Building Society (2017). We
were managing their infrastructure but they were buying
hardware separately through a reseller. When a critical
storage refresh was needed, the reseller quoted 14 weeks
lead time. Our SLA said 4-hour RTO. The maths didn't work.

Since then, we've built a procurement and lifecycle
management capability — not to become a reseller, but to
make sure the things we manage are actually manageable.

--- What We Do ---

Technology Procurement:
· Hardware sourcing (servers, storage, network, end user
  devices) through direct vendor relationships and
  distribution
· Software licensing (Microsoft EA/CSP, VMware, security
  tooling, ITSM)
· Cloud consumption management (Azure CSP Tier 1 partner,
  AWS reseller)
· Competitive procurement (we benchmark pricing quarterly
  against 3 distributors and direct vendor pricing)
· Client passthrough option: We can procure at cost + fixed

  transparency over bundled pricing

--- Commercial Model ---

We offer three procurement commercial models — the client
chooses what suits their financial and governance
preferences:

Model A: Bundled
Hardware, software, and cloud consumption included in the
monthly managed services charge. Single invoice, single
commercial relationship. We own the assets (or lease them)
and wrap them into the service fee. Client pays one number.
Best for: Clients who want opex simplicity.

Model B: Passthrough + Fee
We procure on the client's behalf. Client owns the assets.
We charge cost + handling fee (3-5%). Full transparency on
vendor pricing. Client sees every line item.
Best for: Clients with strong procurement teams who want
control and visibility.

Model C: Hybrid
Cloud consumption and software licensing bundled into
managed services charge (we manage and optimise). Hardware
procured on passthrough basis with lifecycle management
wrapped in.
Best for: Most managed services contracts, including all
current FS clients. This is our recommended model for
Meridian.

We're not trying to be their hardware supplier. We're

6. METHODOLOGY & APPROACH
================================================================

--- Transition Methodology ---

Helios uses a 5-phase transition methodology developed from
14 major transitions:

Phase 1: Discovery & Due Diligence (Months 1-2)
· Detailed service mapping and dependency analysis
· Staff consultation and TUPE assessment
· Tooling audit and integration planning
· Risk register development
· Governance framework agreement

Phase 2: Parallel Run (Months 2-4)
· Shadow operations alongside incumbent/in-house team
· Knowledge transfer (structured, documented, tested)
· Tooling deployment (ServiceNow, monitoring, automation)
· Service desk warm-up (progressive ticket routing)
· DR/BC baseline testing

Phase 3: Controlled Handover (Months 4-6)
· Progressive service transfer (tower by tower)
· Go/no-go gates for each tower
· Hypercare support (enhanced staffing, escalation paths)
· Client satisfaction pulse checks (weekly)
· Risk and issue management (daily standups)

Phase 4: Stabilisation (Months 6-9)
· Full operational ownership
· Performance baseline establishment
· SLA measurement activation
· First CSI cycle
· Lessons learned review

Phase 5: Optimisation (Month 9+)
· Automation pipeline activation
· Cloud migration acceleration
· Innovation fund deployment
· Continuous improvement cadence established

Track record: 14 major transitions completed. Zero P1
incidents during transition. Average time to full BAU: 7
months.

--- Transformation Approach ---

We don't do "big bang" transformation. We do progressive,
evidence-based change:

Principle 1: Run well first, transform second.
We stabilise operations before we change them. Clients who
skip this step end up transforming a mess into a different
mess.

Principle 2: Automate the boring, not the complex.
We start with the 200 manual tasks nobody wants to do — not
the complex workflows that need human judgment. Quick wins
build trust and fund bigger changes.

Principle 3: Measure before and after.
Every transformation initiative has a baseline metric and a
target metric. If we can't measure it, we don't claim it.

Principle 4: Innovation fund is real money, not marketing.
We ring-fence innovation investment (minimum 2% of annual
charge) on a use-it-or-lose-it basis. If we don't spend it,
the client gets it back. This forces us to actually innovate.

--- Why We Don't Lead With Cost ---

Most managed services bids are priced on inputs: headcount
x day rate + tooling + hosting + margin = annual charge.
The client gets a number. The supplier protects the margin.
Nobody talks about what the money actually buys.

Helios prices on value delivered — not effort consumed.

This isn't marketing language. It's how we've structured
our last 6 FS contracts, and it's why our client retention
rate is 94%.

--- The Helios Value Pricing Framework ---

We structure commercial proposals across four pricing layers.
Not every layer applies to every contract — we design the
mix based on what the client values most.

LAYER 1: BASELINE MANAGED SERVICES (Fixed Monthly Charge)

What it covers: The "run" — keeping the lights on, meeting
SLAs, maintaining infrastructure, running the service desk,
managing security. This is the cost of operating the
environment at agreed service levels.

How we price it: Per-unit pricing (per endpoint, per server,
per user) with volume bands. Transparent cost build —
client sees labour, tooling, hosting, overhead, and margin
as separate line items.

Why it matters: This is the foundation. If we can't run the
estate well, nothing else matters. But this layer alone is
commodity pricing — it's where every MSP competes. The
value is in what comes next.

Example (Excalibur):
· Baseline charge: £5.8M/year (6,500 users, 5 towers)
· Equivalent to £74/user/month all-in
· Benchmarked at contract award against 3 competitors
  and independent benchmarking firm (Alsbridge)
· Includes annual indexation (CPI-linked, capped at 4%)

LAYER 2: OUTCOME-LINKED PAYMENTS (Performance Bonus Pool)

What it covers: Payments linked to achieving defined
business outcomes — not just SLA targets, but things the
client's Board actually cares about.

How it works: A portion of the annual charge (typically
5-8%) is held in a performance pool. Helios earns it by
hitting outcome targets agreed annually. If we miss, the
client keeps the money. If we exceed, we earn additional
bonus from a shared upside fund.

The difference from service credits: Service credits
penalise failure. Outcome-linked payments reward success.
Both matter, but the psychology is different — we're
incentivised to over-deliver, not just avoid under-
delivering.

Typical outcome metrics we've priced against:

Operational:
· Infrastructure availability (weighted by business
  criticality, not flat %)
· Mean time to restore critical services (hours, not days)
· Service desk FCR improvement trajectory (not just a
  static target)
· Automation adoption rate (% of eligible processes
  automated)
· Employee IT satisfaction score improvement

Business:
· Reduction in micro-outage cost (£ saved vs. baseline)
· Regulatory reporting timeliness (Consumer Duty MI
  delivered within X hours, not the current 72)
· Audit findings reduction (fewer IT-related audit points
  year-on-year)
· Cloud migration milestone delivery (on-time, measured
  quarterly)

Example (Excalibur):
· Performance pool: £420K/year (7.2% of baseline charge)
· 6 outcome metrics agreed annually with CIO
· Year 1 result: Helios earned 91% of pool (£382K)
· Metrics that drove it: FCR improvement (54%→67%), infra
  availability (99.92%→99.97%), 220 processes automated
· Metric we missed: Cloud migration milestone Q2 (delayed
  6 weeks due to legacy application dependency — client
  acknowledged shared responsibility, partial payment
  agreed)

Example (Athena):
· Performance pool: £280K/year
· Key outcome metric: DR recovery time — contract required
  us to demonstrate 4-hour RTO for critical systems
  annually. We hit 3h 42m in FY2025 test. Full pool earned.

LAYER 3: GAIN-SHARE (Shared Value Creation)

What it covers: When Helios's work creates measurable
financial value for the client beyond the contracted scope
— cost savings, revenue enablement, risk reduction — we
share the upside.

How it works: Gain-share requires three things:

1. Agreed baseline (independently verified where possible)
2. Agreed measurement methodology (before we start, not
   after we've delivered)
3. Transparent accounting (both sides see the numbers)

Typical gain-share structures:
· Cost savings: 50:50 split of verified savings above
  an agreed baseline
· Risk reduction: Shared benefit from reduced insurance
  premiums, regulatory fines avoided, or incident costs
  averted (harder to measure, but we've done it)
· Revenue enablement: Where IT improvements directly
  enable revenue (e.g., faster onboarding, better uptime
  for trading platforms) — typically 20:80 (Helios:client)

What we've actually paid out (UK FS contracts):

  Excalibur FY2025:    £180K (from infrastructure cost

                       right-sizing, licence reclamation)
  Athena FY2025:       £420K (from DR cost reduction —

  Cavendish FY2025:    £95K (from automation of manual

  TrustMark FY2025:    £210K (from cloud optimisation —

Total gain-share paid to UK FS clients in FY2025: £905K

Why this matters: Gain-share only works if the supplier is
genuinely motivated to find savings — not just protect
existing revenue. Our model works because we price the
baseline sustainably (Layer 1) and earn upside through
performance (Layer 2) and value creation (Layer 3). We
don't need to protect bloated baselines because our base
margin is already healthy.
