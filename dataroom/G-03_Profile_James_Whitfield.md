# G-03: EXECUTIVE INTELLIGENCE — James Whitfield, Chief Executive Officer
## Meridian Financial Services — The Distracted Visionary

## SCQA
**SITUATION:** James Whitfield is the CEO, appointed 2019. Architect of the £16.5M 'Meridian 2030' strategy. Over 20 years in UK financial services leadership. His guiding mantra is 'Availability, not assets'.

**COMPLICATION:** Whitfield is a visionary leader whose public narrative has completely detached from operational reality. While he sells 'cloud-first agility' to investors, his internal operations are plagued by 62% EOL servers, a 47-minute incident response gap, and a hostile Audit Committee that has frozen the IT budget.

**ANSWER:** Do not sell 'vision' or 'transformation' to James Whitfield; sell him the operational proof and risk mitigation required to save his Meridian 2030 legacy from the CFO's budget cuts.

## KEY PROFILE DATA
- **Primary GAIN Driver:** ACHIEVEMENT
- **Authority:** 100/100
- **Influence:** 85 (Checked by Board)/100
- **Kegan Stage:** Stage 4 (Self-Authoring)
- **Executive Playbook:** Visionary / Top-Down Strategy
- **Vendor Loyalty Check:** None

## CORRIDOR TEST
He is a visionary CEO selling a cloud-first narrative while his internal C-suite is at war over a frozen budget and sparking servers.

**THE ONE THING:** He cares deeply about how the market perceives Meridian 2030; anything that threatens that narrative is a problem.

**THE ONE LANDMINE:** Assuming that because he is the CEO, he can automatically unlock frozen IT capital. He cannot bypass his Audit Chair.
