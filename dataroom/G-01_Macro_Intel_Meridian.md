## EXECUTIVE SCQA

**SITUATION:** Meridian Financial Services is a mid-tier UK financial institution (£14.2bn AUM) experiencing strong top-line growth (Revenue £847m, +6.2% YoY) but suffering from severe legacy IT debt where 62% of servers are end-of-life.

**COMPLICATION:** The CEO's public "Meridian 2030" transformation strategy (£16.5m annual budget) is actively being starved by the CFO and Board Audit Committee. A sudden switch of auditors to EY signals a capitalization crackdown, effectively freezing net-new IT OPEX. Meanwhile, the PRA has issued formal Section 166 warnings regarding third-party risk, and micro-outages are bleeding £2.8m annually.

**QUESTION:** How do we penetrate a Tier-1 financial institution where the executive board is at war, the transformation budget is frozen, and our seller is chasing a £2.5M phantom software deal?

**ANSWER:** Abandon the standalone software pitch immediately. Re-position the capability as a mandatory, risk-mitigating subcontractor requirement within their recently issued 7-year, £115M Managed IT Services RFP (MFS-2026-ORT-001).
