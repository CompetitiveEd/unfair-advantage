# G-02: FINANCIAL FORENSICS & COMMERCIAL LEVERS — Meridian Financial Services

## EXECUTIVE SCQA (Financial Lens)

**SITUATION:** Meridian is reporting healthy top-line growth with Revenue at £847m (+6.2% YoY) and Operating Profit at £287m. They have publicly committed £16.5m annually to their "Meridian 2030" tech transformation.

**COMPLICATION:** The top-line health is masking severe margin decay. Legacy IT infrastructure (micro-outages) caused a 12% increase in unplanned operational costs this year. To combat OPEX bloat, the CFO has brought in EY to crack down on software capitalization, effectively freezing the CIO's net-new budget. Concurrently, Procurement is trying to shift this financial risk off the balance sheet entirely via a £115m outcome-linked managed services RFP.

**QUESTION:** How do we extract a £2.5M commercial victory from an account where the CFO has frozen capital expenditure and is actively pushing risk to third-party outsourcers?

**ANSWER:** Stop pitching software as a net-new capital expense. Restructure the commercial offer to map directly to the 50:50 gain-share and 15% at-risk provisions of the upcoming RFP, proving that our automation cleanly eliminates the £2.8M micro-outage OPEX bleed.

## PHASE 1: THE P&L TEARDOWN & MARGIN PANIC

- **The Top Line:** Gross Written Premium is up 8.2% to £3.1bn. Revenue sits at £847m.
- **The Efficiency Gap:** Analysts note Meridian's SG&A is 220 basis points higher than peer Oakstone Financial. This is the CFO's "Bleeding Neck."
- **The Micro-Outage Tax:** Legacy infrastructure friction resulted in a 12% spike in unplanned operational costs.

## PHASE 2: THE CAPEX VS. OPEX BOARDROOM WAR

**The CIO's Dilemma:** CIO Nicholas Reid wants to build "Project Citadel" (Cloud Migration). IT build projects are typically capitalized (CAPEX), meaning the cost is amortized over several years, protecting current-year EBITDA.

**The CFO's Weapon:** CFO Mark Thompson (an EY alumnus) replaced the auditor to force a restatement of IT costs. If EY determines the cloud migration is just "maintenance" of legacy systems, those costs instantly become OPEX.

**The Commercial Reality:** Thompson has engineered a scenario where Reid literally cannot sign a £2.5M software deal without destroying the bank's quarterly operating margin. The budget is frozen.

## PHASE 3: RFP FINANCIAL DECODING (MFS-2026-ORT-001)

| RFP Commercial Clause | What It Actually Means | How We Exploit It |
|---|---|---|
| Outcome-linked pricing mechanisms | Meridian will not pay for "effort" or software licenses; they only pay for uptime. | We price our software based on the reduction of the £2.8M micro-outage SLA penalties. |
| 50:50 Gain-Share above baseline | The SI vendor gets a bonus if they make Meridian more efficient. | We approach the bidding Tier-1 SIs and show them our tool guarantees their gain-share bonus. |
| 15% At-Risk provisions | If the SI vendor causes an outage, they lose 15% of their revenue. | Our automated dependency mapping is the insurance policy that protects the SI's 15% revenue at risk. |
| 2% Innovation Fund (Use-it-or-lose-it) | £2.3M is ring-fenced purely for "innovation" by the winning vendor. | This is our target funding. We become the designated "Innovation Fund" spend for the winning SI. |

## PHASE 4: EXECUTIVE COMPENSATION ALIGNMENT

- **CEO James Whitfield:** LTIP heavily weighted toward EBITDA margin expansion. He talks about transformation, but his bonus relies on the CFO ruthlessly managing costs.
- **CFO Mark Thompson:** Bonus tied to Return on Equity (currently 14.7%). He will kill any project that threatens this metric.
- **Alignment Conclusion:** You cannot sell "resilience" to the C-suite if it looks like an expensive insurance policy. You must sell it as Margin Protection.

## PHASE 5: COST OF INACTION QUANTIFICATION

- **Direct OPEX Bleed:** £2.8M annually from micro-outage resolution.
- **Regulatory Fines (PRA):** Unquantifiable, but Section 166 enforcement is highly disruptive.
- **Outsourcing Transition Failure:** If they hand over 62% EOL servers to an SI without mapping them first, the SI will trigger massive "Change Request" fees. Estimated at 10-15% of total contract value (£11M - £17M penalty).
- **Total Financial Risk: £13.8M - £19.8M in Year 1 alone.**

## THE ONE-PAGER: G-02 FINANCIAL VERDICT

**SITUATION:** Meridian has strong revenue (£847m) but is experiencing acute OPEX bloat due to legacy IT failures (+12% unplanned costs).

**COMPLICATION:** The CFO has weaponized an EY audit to freeze the CIO's capital expenditure, meaning your seller's £2.5M Q2 standalone software deal is financially impossible to sign. Simultaneously, Procurement is shifting all IT financial risk to a £115M managed services RFP.

**ANSWER:** Abandon the direct £2.5M CAPEX software pitch. Pivot entirely to a "Subcontractor/Innovation Fund" play.

**THE FINANCIAL LEVERS:**
1. **The 2% Innovation Fund:** The £115M RFP ring-fences a 2% "use-it-or-lose-it" innovation fund. This equates to exactly £2.3M. This is where your budget lives.
2. **The 15% At-Risk Protection:** We sell our platform to the Tier-1 SIs bidding on the RFP as an "insurance policy" to protect their 15% revenue at risk.
3. **The OPEX Relief:** We position our automation to the CFO as the direct cure for the £2.8M micro-outage bleed.

**CORRIDOR TEST:** The internal budget is frozen; our only path to revenue is capturing the 2% innovation fund inside the £115M outsourcing contract.
