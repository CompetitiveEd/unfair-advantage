THE NAVIGATION LAYER

The AI is the Engine. You own the Flight Plan.

Speed without direction is just a faster way to get lost. Prompts are the controls, and you are the pilot.

THE DRIFT

Unconscious Automation

A script makes you robotic. AI agents negotiating with AI agents. No room for instinct, rapport, or timing.

"Write me an email to the CFO." -> Result: Ignored.

THE STEER

Active Intelligence

Preparation makes you confident. 4 hours of AI research so you walk in informed and fully present.

"Act as a cynical CFO..." -> Result: Advantage.

THE FLIGHT PLAN

Human In The Loop

The prompt chain looks linear but you don't run it like a pipeline. Between every step, you read the output, add your own context, and adjust. This principle applies across all four GAIN phases — Gather, Assess, Influence, Nurture — not just Week 1. The flight plan below uses Week 1 as the example, but the human-in-the-loop discipline is the same throughout.

STEP 01

Company Intel

Establish a baseline. Strategic goals, annual report themes, stated risks.

STEP 02

Find Money

Scan for budget signals. Regional disparities, transformation funds.

STEP 03

Profile People

Map influence. Who holds the budget? Who is the blocker?

STEP 04

Battle Card

Generate strategy. A "Why Us, Why Now" narrative.

STEP 05

Your Company

Align capabilities to pain. Use their internal terminology.

STEP 06

The Briefing

The final output. Proves you know their business.

WHAT EXPERIENCE SEES

The Manchester insight came from a corridor conversation, not from AI. That's the point. The most valuable context you'll ever add to a prompt is something no search engine could find — an offhand comment from a project manager, a rumour at an industry event, a pattern you've seen three times before. AI can't have that conversation for you. But once you have it, AI can turn it into strategy in minutes.

SIGNS OF DECAY

  • Repetition: The Battle Card looks just like Step 3.
  • Amnesia: "Who is the stakeholder again?"
  • Hallucination: Inventing facts not in provided source material.

THE FIX

Don't argue with a degrading context window. Start a fresh chat and use the Handoff Prompt.

CHAT HANDOFF — CONTEXT TRANSFER
I am continuing our previous research. Please acknowledge that you have the context from our earlier steps before we proceed.

PRE-FLIGHT

Before You Begin

START FAMILIAR

Your first run will feel slow. Pick a prospect you know. Compare output to what's in your head.

DON'T EDIT

Run the prompt as written first. The structure is deliberate. Edit later.

THREE ATTEMPTS

Give it three runs before judging. The third is where it clicks.

WHERE THIS BREAKS

Wrong Context In

You steer the AI toward Manchester because you heard about the skills gap — but your information was six months old. The gap was filled. The AI builds an entire strategy around a problem that no longer exists. The stakeholder wonders if you've done any real research at all.

RULE: Verify your human intel before injecting it. Stale context is worse than no context.

Over-Steering

You're so certain the CTO is the decision-maker that you steer every prompt toward technical priorities. The AI confirms your hypothesis beautifully — because you told it to. Meanwhile, the CFO is the actual economic buyer and nobody's mapped their concerns. Steering becomes confirmation bias.

RULE: Steer the research, not the conclusion. Let the AI surprise you.
