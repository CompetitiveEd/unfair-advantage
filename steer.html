FIELD-TESTED ACROSS £183M

The governance principles on this page aren't theoretical. They're the operating rhythm that transformed a £35M portfolio into £183M, delivered £15M contribution against a £10.9M plan, and scaled a team from 7 to 11 specialist sellers without losing margin.

The Four Imperatives

Long-term commercial governance isn't a single KPI. It's a balanced scorecard across growth, efficiency, retention, and innovation.

IMPERATIVE 01

Lead & Grow

Healthy contracts, managed risk

The Why: Pursue growth, but not at any cost. Walk away from contracts with structural risk.

Measure: Forward order book, modelled vs actual performance, win ratio.

IMPERATIVE 02

Productivity & Competitiveness

Standard capability, proactive disruption

The Why: Leverage standard offerings before building custom. Disrupt before being disrupted.

Measure: Analyst feedback, relevant margin to service, bid efficiency.

IMPERATIVE 03

Retention & Maximisation

Existing clients, new energy

The Why: Your installed base is your cheapest growth engine. Feed it constantly.

Measure: Erosion tracking, renewal rates, podium elevation (share of wallet).

IMPERATIVE 04

Innovation

New models, new narratives

The Why: The way you won the last deal won't win the next one. Evolve constantly.

Measure: Customer NPS, contract base expansion, innovation revenue.

THE FLIGHT PLAN

Human In The Loop

The prompt chain looks linear but you don't run it like a pipeline. Between every step, you read the output, add your own context, and adjust. This principle applies across all four GAIN phases — Gather, Assess, Influence, Nurture — not just Week 1.

STEP 01

Company Intel

Establish a baseline. Strategic goals, annual report themes, stated risks.

STEP 02

Find Money

Scan for budget signals. Regional disparities, transformation funds.

STEP 03

Profile People

Map influence. Who holds the budget? Who is the blocker?

STEP 04

Battle Card

Generate strategy. A "Why Us, Why Now" narrative.

STEP 05

Your Company

Align capabilities to pain. Use their internal terminology.

STEP 06

The Briefing

The final output. Proves you know their business.

WHERE THIS BREAKS

Wrong Context In

You steer the AI toward Manchester because you heard about the skills gap — but your information was six months old. The gap was filled. The AI builds an entire strategy around a problem that no longer exists.

RULE: Verify your human intel before injecting it.

Over-Steering

You're so certain the CTO is the decision-maker that you steer every prompt toward technical priorities. The AI confirms your hypothesis beautifully — because you told it to. Steering becomes confirmation bias.

RULE: Steer the research, not the conclusion.
