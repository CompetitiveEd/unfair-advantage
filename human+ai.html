THE FLIGHT PLAN

You don't take off without knowing the destination, the fuel, the weather, and the landing strip. The same discipline applies here.

PRE-FLIGHT 01

Know Your Destination

What decision are you trying to influence? A renewal, a new logo, an expansion? The prompts adapt — but only if you tell them where you're going. Define the outcome before you start.

PRE-FLIGHT 02

Check Your Fuel

What data do you already have? Annual reports, LinkedIn profiles, delivery team intelligence, corridor conversations. The more you bring, the sharper the output. AI fills gaps — it doesn't replace what you know.

PRE-FLIGHT 03

Check the Weather

Is the deal environment stable or turbulent? Restructuring, leadership changes, regulatory pressure — these change the flight path. Check before you build strategy on assumptions.

PRE-FLIGHT 04

Plan the Return

Winning the deal is the outbound flight. Protecting the contract is the return. GAIN covers both — Gather through Influence gets you there. Nurture gets you home. Don't plan one without the other.

Start familiar. Run your first prompts on a prospect you already know. Compare the output to what's in your head. You'll see intelligence you had (the tool works) and intelligence you missed (the tool adds value). Don't edit the prompt before you've tried it as written. Give yourself three attempts before judging.

What this looks like in practice: You run Step 1 on a prospect. The AI returns 9 pillars of research. Pillar 7 flags a "resilience skills gap." You already know — because you spoke to someone inside the company last week — that the gap is specifically in their Manchester office and it's causing friction with London leadership.

You don't just move to Step 2. You modify it: "Pay particular attention to skills-gap-related costs, especially regional disparities." Now Step 2's output is sharper. That insight carries through to your briefing as a specific observation that makes the stakeholder think: "How did they know that?"

The answer: they didn't get it from AI. They got it from experience, fed it into AI, and let the system amplify it.

WHEN THE CONVERSATION BREAKS

Every AI conversation has a shelf life. When the context window fills, the system silently drops your earlier content. It doesn't warn you.

Repetition

You ask for a battle card and the output looks like something it generated three prompts ago.

Forgotten Context

You mentioned the stakeholder's name in Step 1. By Step 5, the AI uses a generic placeholder.

Generic Output

Early outputs were specific and detailed. Later outputs could apply to any company in any industry.

Hallucination

Facts start appearing that weren't in any source material you provided.

When you see any of these: start a new chat. Use the handoff prompt.

CHAT HANDOFF — CONTEXT TRANSFER
I'm continuing research on [COMPANY NAME] for a meeting with [STAKEHOLDER NAME, TITLE]. Here's what I've established so far: COMPANY CONTEXT: [Paste the 3 most important findings] STAKEHOLDER PROFILE: [Key details: role, decision-making style, priorities] BUDGET SIGNALS: [Top 2-3 budget findings] WHAT I NEED NEXT: [The specific step or task you're continuing with] CONTEXT I'VE ADDED FROM MY OWN KNOWLEDGE: [Personal observations, adjustments to AI output] Continue from here. Do not re-research what I've provided — build on it.
CHOOSING YOUR TOOLS

The prompts work in any AI that takes natural language. The methodology is platform-independent. Three principles:

Different tools surface different things. Run the same prompt in two AI systems — you'll get different outputs. That's a feature. Use multiple tools when the stakes are high.

Start free. Upgrade when outputs go generic. Free-tier tools work with every prompt. What changes with paid is the depth and context window. You'll know when you've hit the ceiling.

Read the output critically, not gratefully. AI produces confident, well-structured text regardless of accuracy. Your job is to verify, not to be impressed.

WHERE THIS BREAKS

Wrong Context In

You steer the AI toward Manchester because you heard about the skills gap — but your info was old. The AI builds a strategy for a problem that no longer exists.

RULE: Verify your human intel before injecting it.

Over-Steering

You're so certain the CTO is the decision-maker that you steer every prompt toward technical priorities. The AI confirms your bias beautifully.

RULE: Steer the research, not the conclusion.
