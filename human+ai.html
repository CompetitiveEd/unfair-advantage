04. WHERE THIS BREAKS

THE DRIFT (Unconscious)

A script makes you robotic. AI agents negotiating with AI agents. No room for instinct, rapport, or timing. "Write me an email to the CFO" → Result: Ignored.

IQ+EQ+AI (Active)

Preparation makes you confident. 4 hours of AI research so you walk in informed and fully present. "Act as a cynical CFO..." → Result: Advantage.

Wrong Context In

You steer the AI toward Manchester because you heard about the skills gap — but your info was old. The AI builds a strategy for a problem that no longer exists.

RULE: Verify your human intel before injecting it.

Over-Steering

You're so certain the CTO is the decision-maker that you steer every prompt toward technical priorities. The AI confirms your bias beautifully.

RULE: Steer the research, not the conclusion.
05. TACTICAL UTILITIES
  • 01

    Different tools surface different things. Run the same prompt in two AI systems — you'll get different outputs. That's a feature. Use multiple tools when the stakes are high.

  • 02

    Start free. Upgrade when outputs go generic. Free-tier tools work with every prompt. What changes with paid is the depth and context window. You'll know when you've hit the ceiling.

  • 03

    Read the output critically, not gratefully. AI produces confident, well-structured text regardless of accuracy. Your job is to verify, not to be impressed.

  • 04

    Sanitise the Input. Never upload proprietary pricing, unannounced M&A data, or PII into public AI models. Use abstractions. AI is for public intelligence synthesis, not secure data storage.

When the context window fills, the AI drops earlier content. If outputs become repetitive or hallucinate facts, use the handoff protocol.
