A Founder Who Couldn't Leave
Boutique technology firm. £9M revenue. One person winning every deal. Sellers maintaining accounts, not growing them. The business was the founder. The founder was the business.
3-year exit plan. Business self-sustaining. The multiple changes even if revenue doesn't.

The Diagnosis

Sellers without licence to act. No capability infrastructure — no account intelligence, no whitespace mapping. Motivation driven by fear, not purpose.

The Fix

Structural, not training. New sales leadership. Restructured finance for decision-quality data. Pivot to customer intimacy.

The Result

Founder makes judgment calls with time to think. Sales team doubled. 75/76 event attendance. Founder on 3-year exit. Business runs without him.

Where This Breaks
The Middle Management Gap

The methodology relies on aligning with C-Suite financial mandates. If your team is too terrified to bypass the VP of IT or the Procurement Lead, the strategy will fail. Middle managers buy features; boards buy risk mitigation.

The Confidence Problem

You can give a team the perfect AI-generated executive dossier. But if they lack the presence to walk into a boardroom and challenge a CFO's worldview, the data is useless. Tools do not equal talent.
