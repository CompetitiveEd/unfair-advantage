THE PROOF — EVIDENCE OF WORK

THREE TRANSFORMATIONS. SAME PLAYBOOK.

The numbers behind the methodology.

In each case, the starting conditions were identical: strong propositions, talented people, weak execution. Messaging diluted. Every deal reliant on hero-effort. No shared language. No system.

The diagnostic was always the same. The methodology was always the same. Only the speed has changed.

PROOF OF WORK 01 — THE GO-TO-MARKET

A professional services unit inside a FTSE-listed technology company. Business consensus: it loses money. Leadership wanted it gone.

The breakthrough was a single folded piece of paper. Twelve steps a customer needed for a successful migration. Not what the business sold — what the customer needed. When pitched, the question wasn't 'buy our services.' It was: 'here's the journey — what can you do, what can we do?' It became the most successful proposition in the business. It went viral. It became standard.

The team was built from scratch. Non-traditional hires. An analyst retrained into sales. A software specialist who'd never touched services became a top performer. Recognition was quarterly, not annual. The culture attracted talent that conventional recruitment missed.

PROOF OF WORK 03 — THE PEOPLE MULTIPLIER

A neglected enterprise maintenance business. Under-resourced. Under-invested. Every stone uncovered a problem.

Five-day diagnostic. Interviewed everyone — delivery, project, sales, account, sector, marketing. Found unstructured lost cost across twelve operational elements. Identified a multi-billion addressable market at single-digit penetration. Then rebuilt the team — not by hiring experienced sellers, but by giving undervalued people a system.

The Engineer

A senior technical professional on a comfortable salary. Didn't want sales. Took honest persuasion over months. Within two years: enterprise deals exceeding £10M annually. Margins above 50%.

The Administrator

A renewals back-office role. Underrated. Afraid of his own potential. Moved into a dedicated client-facing position with a system behind him. Generated more individual revenue than anyone in the group's history. Won the organisation's highest performance award. Repeated it the following year.

The Culture

Quarterly recognition, not annual. A signed baseball bat for the home run. A cap for the quarter leader. The team went from zero pipeline to consistent over-achievement across the group.

These people didn't suddenly become talented. They always were. The system gave them structure. The methodology gave them confidence. The leadership gave them permission.

WHERE THIS BREAKS

The Middle Management Gap

The system was underestimated at the middle layer. Account directors adopted it. Team leaders reverted to old habits. It worked when the architect was in the room. This is why GAIN's Grow phase focuses on Commercial Governance — the operating rhythm that ensures the methodology outlives any individual.

The Confidence Problem

Not every person responds to a system. Some sellers define themselves by instinct and resist structure on principle. The methodology doesn't fix ego. It works for people who want to be better — not people convinced they already are.
